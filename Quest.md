# Quest

A **Quest** is a tangible act of goodwill that a [[Circles|Circle]] undertakes together — real work that makes life measurably more like [[Heaven On Earth]], right here and now.

It is not a discussion. It is not a plan. It is [[Meaningful Work]] — something you can point to and say: *we did this together, and the world is better for it.*

___

## What Makes a Quest

A Quest should be:

- **Real** — it actually transforms your lives, relationships, or community
- **Achievable** — within reach with several hours per week from your Circle
- **Meaningful** — connected to needs you see and gifts you can offer
- **Together** — requires collaboration, not individual action
- **Seasonal** — aligned to the ~90-day rhythm between solstices and equinoxes

___

## The Sacred Dimension

In the great traditions, a Quest is a journey toward something that matters — often infused with sacred, symbolic, or allegorical meaning. Tales of Quests figure prominently in the folklore of every tribe, nation, and tradition.

In [[The Great Game of LIONSBERG]], every Quest carries this dual nature: it is both a practical project and a spiritual practice. The outer work of transforming your community is inseparable from the inner work of transforming yourselves.

Every Quest accomplished is a move in [[The Great Game of LIONSBERG|The Great Game]]. Every Circle completing its Quest and passing [[The Flame]] advances the whole. All Quests — from the smallest local act to the greatest planetary endeavor — stack up and align toward [[The Goal]]: the progressive realization of Heaven On Earth.

___

## Examples

- Plant a community garden in a neglected space
- Create a mutual aid network for elders or families in need
- Build or repair something needed locally
- Teach a skill to youth
- Map and begin a directory of local offers and needs
- Host a neighborhood gathering to build connection
- Document local wisdom from long-time residents or indigenous elders
- Clear and maintain a local trail or natural space
