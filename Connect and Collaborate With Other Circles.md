# Connect and Collaborate With Other Circles

_You are not alone in this.  
Across your city, your region, your world —  
other Circles are forming, other Quests are underway.  
The movement grows when these living cells find each other._

---

## Make Your Circle Visible

The first step is the simplest: **let others know you exist.**

Share your Circle's name, your [[Quest]], your general location, and a way to reach you in the [[Commons]] — the shared directory where Circles, Guilds, and communities make themselves known.

You do not need to share everything. You need to share enough — enough for another Circle to recognize shared purpose and reach out.

A Circle that remains invisible remains isolated. A Circle that makes itself visible becomes a node in a growing network.

---

## Discover Other Circles

Finding other Circles is not a bureaucratic process. It is organic. It is relational.

- **Explore the directory** — browse the Commons for Circles near you or aligned with your domain
- **Ask your network** — the people in your Circle already know people in other Circles, even if they do not yet realize it
- **Attend seasonal gatherings** — regional and global convocations where Circles come together for celebration, retrospection, and renewal
- **Follow the Quests** — when you see work being done in your community that resonates, trace it back to its source; there is almost certainly a Circle behind it
- **[[Pass The Flame]]** — every time someone passes the invitation forward, new connections form naturally

---

## Establish Communication

Once you find another Circle, establish a channel. It does not need to be sophisticated. It needs to be reliable and trusted.

- **In-person** — nothing replaces face-to-face contact; meet for a meal, a walk, a shared work day
- **Simple messaging** — a shared Signal group, a Matrix channel, a text thread
- **Regular rhythm** — a monthly check-in between coordinators is enough to maintain living connection
- **Honesty from the start** — share what you are working on, what you need, what you can offer

Communication between Circles is not reporting. It is relationship. Treat it as you would a friendship between households — warm, honest, practical, and sustained over time.

---

## Collaborate on Quests

This is where connection becomes co-creation.

When two or more Circles discover complementary Quests — where one Circle's strengths meet another Circle's needs, or where separate efforts would be more powerful combined — collaboration becomes natural.

- **Identify overlap** — where are your Quests headed in the same direction?
- **Share resources** — tools, knowledge, contacts, labor, funding
- **Coordinate timing** — align your rhythms so that efforts reinforce rather than conflict
- **Offer and request help** — a Circle that asks for help is not weak; it is wise
- **Celebrate together** — shared victories deepen the bonds between Circles

Collaboration is not merger. Each Circle retains its sovereignty, its own [[Field of Agreements]], its own internal life. Collaboration is two living cells working side by side — each whole, each contributing something the other cannot.

---

## The Movement Grows Through Trust

The Great Game does not grow through central coordination, marketing campaigns, or institutional directives. It grows through **trust-based networks of Circles recognizing shared purpose**.

One Circle finds another. They share a meal. They discover their Quests are complementary. They begin to coordinate. Their members pass the Flame to others. New Circles form. The network thickens.

This is how 3 become 13 become 144 become millions.

Not through control. Through connection.  
Not through hierarchy. Through resonance.  
Not through force. Through the ancient, organic mathematics of trust.

**Reach out. Make yourself known. Find the others. The Body is assembling itself — and your Circle is part of it.**
