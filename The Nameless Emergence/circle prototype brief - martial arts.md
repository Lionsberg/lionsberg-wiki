# circle prototype brief - martial arts
*demonstrating specialized practice circles at the most intimate scale*

---

## purpose

The martial arts circle demonstrates what a specialized practice circle looks like when actually implemented.

This is **not** primarily about martial arts. It's about:
- Testing circle protocols in real conditions
- Documenting what works for other specialized circles to replicate
- Modeling that leaders are participants, not above the game
- Proving that weekly practice builds transformation over time

The martial arts theme provides a specific practice container. Other circles will focus on healing, study, food, governance, building, etc. The **pattern** is what matters.

## structure

**Size:** 3-12 practitioners (optimal 4-8)

**Frequency:** Weekly gathering, 90-120 minutes

**Location:** [North Idaho location TBD]

**Led by:** The Nameless One (as participant-facilitator, not instructor-authority)

**Open to:** Those who resonate with the practice and commit to regular attendance

## what we actually do

### Opening (10 minutes)
- Arrive and transition from daily life
- Brief centering practice (breath, stillness, remembering who we are)
- Check-in round: Each person shares where they are (1-2 minutes each)
- Set intention for the practice

### Physical Practice (45-60 minutes)
Rotating focus across traditional martial disciplines:
- **Forms and kata** - Traditional movement sequences from various martial arts
- **Chi Gong / Energy work** - Cultivation of internal energy and awareness
- **Sparring / Partner drills** - Controlled application with safety and respect
- **Breathwork** - Advanced breathing techniques for energy and presence
- **Strength and conditioning** - Physical preparation for the path

### Philosophy and Integration (20-30 minutes)
- Discussion of warrior ethics, guardianship principles
- Integration with The Sacred Ascent framework
- Application to daily life and The Great Game
- Questions, insights, challenges

### Closing (10 minutes)
- Brief reflection: What did we discover today?
- Commitments for the week ahead
- Gratitude round
- Closing ritual

## why martial arts

**Embodies core principles:**
- Discipline and continuous improvement
- Balance of individual excellence with collective training
- Integration of physical, mental, spiritual dimensions
- Practical skills for protection during turbulent times
- Sacred warrior path across all traditions

**Tests the pattern:**
- Can circles meet consistently over months?
- Do protocols work for specialized practice?
- Does weekly rhythm build genuine transformation?
- Can documentation enable replication?

**Models leadership:**
- The Nameless One participates as member, not guru
- Everyone contributes based on their experience
- Leadership rotates for different focuses
- Authority comes from embodiment, not position

## commitments required

### From participants:
1. **Regular attendance** - Missing occasionally is fine, chronic absence doesn't work
2. **Full presence** - When here, be here (not on phone, not distracted)
3. **Mutual respect** - Honor each person's level and journey
4. **Safety first** - In sparring and physical practice, control and care essential
5. **Integration** - Apply insights to daily life and The Great Game
6. **Invitation** - When ready, invite 1-2 others who would benefit

### From the circle as whole:
1. **Document everything** - Protocols, practices, what works, what doesn't
2. **Contribute to playbook** - Feed learnings to The Nameless Playbook
3. **Welcome newcomers** - Integrate new members smoothly
4. **Maintain sacred container** - This is practice, not social hour
5. **Replicate when ready** - Support others starting their own martial circles
