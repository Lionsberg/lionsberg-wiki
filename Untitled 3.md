In 1969, a graduate student named Steve Crocker wrote a document he was embarrassed to publish. He and his colleagues were building something none of them fully understood — a network connecting computers across several universities. They didn't know the right answers. So instead of publishing a specification, Crocker published a _question_. He called it a "Request for Comments." The name signaled humility: _we are not authorities. We are fellow builders who need help seeing what we cannot see alone._

From that posture — rough consensus and running code FAST — the internet emerged.

This document follows the same tradition, adapted for a different scale. It is not a specification. It is a structured invitation to **think and act together** around what may be the most consequential coordination challenge in human history:

This document attempts to map that convergence and ask for the guidance of those who can see more clearly.

# TECHNICAL ADDENDUM: THE INFRASTRUCTURE MAP

_For builders, protocol designers, data architects, and anyone who wants to understand what is already converging beneath the surface._

### Identity and Trust

The **First Person Network** — born from Linux Foundation Decentralized Trust, Trust Over IP, the Decentralized Identity Foundation, and OpenWallet Foundation — has developed decentralized, privacy-preserving proof of personhood through Personhood Credentials (issued by communities that know you) and Verifiable Relationship Credentials (issued peer-to-peer between people who know each other). No central database. No biometric harvesting. Zero-knowledge proof verification. A network cooperative (the First Person Cooperative) is being established as governance.

The **Internet Identity Workshop** community — meeting twice yearly since 2005 — has spent 20 years building the foundational standards (DIDs, Verifiable Credentials, digital wallets, the Trust Over IP protocol stack) upon which this rests.

**The human-level protocol:** A Circle of people who know each other in person IS a trust graph. The grandmother verified by her neighbors needs no digital credential. One trained person per Circle bridges the digital and human trust layers. No one is involuntarily left behind.

**Guidance needed:** Fastest path to operational integration between decentralized identity infrastructure and Circle-based trust structures. How to bootstrap trust networks before the infrastructure is mature.

---

### InterFace Protocol

Every sovereign entity — individual, Circle, community, bioregion — needs a way to manage what it shares, with whom, and under what conditions.

**IFP (InterFace Protocol)** — Each sovereignty has an Interface mediated by an agent (AI or human) managing identity, disclosure tiers, and trust gradients. Designed so core principles can be executed on paper, in person, or with best available agentic technology. 

**AT Protocol / Atmosphere** — the protocol underlying Bluesky, expanding beyond social media. Handles discovery and public expression. Complementary to IFP: AT Proto for public, IFP for private trust-bounded communication.

**Trust Over IP (ToIP) Protocol Stack** — a four-layer trust protocol modeled on TCP/IP. Twenty years of standards work.

**Key insight:** The rules of protocol design change fundamentally when intelligent agents mediate. Agents can negotiate, adapt, switch protocols in real time. But protocols must degrade gracefully into human-manageable steps — executable on paper if necessary.

**Guidance needed:** Formal connection between IFP and First Person. Disclosure tier specification that is both machine-readable and human-comprehensible. Agent mediation without new exploitation vectors.

---

### Privacy and Sovereignty Enforcement

If an AI agent must understand you deeply enough to serve you, that agent becomes the richest target for any adversary. Mathematical and architectural guarantees must ensure no single system can reconstruct the full behavioral profile of a sovereign being. Privacy as architecture, not policy.

Independent researchers are developing approaches based on structural separation of conflicting constraints — delegation and protection must not be collapsed into a single system. Bilateral agreement protocols, zero-knowledge proofs, and information-theoretic bounds on reconstruction. Connected to IEEE 7012, Trust Over IP, and the broader standards ecosystem.

---

### Holofractal Data Architecture

**Current** — a holofractal data architecture with 10+ years of development. Sovereign data spaces at every level — individual, Circle, community, bioregion, planetary grid. Tested in compliance-demanding contexts (FAA-compliant operations, university systems).

**Open Impact** — built "API-up" on Current. SDK documentation underway. LIØNSBERG funded the opening sprint (2021-2024) through the Open Future Coalition.

**CRDTs** (Conflict-Free Replicated Data Types) — each person edits on their own device, all copies eventually converge. Essential for offline-first operation. Leading implementations: Automerge, Yjs.

**Oasis** — another holofractal data architecture recently brought into dialogue.

**Guidance needed:** Governance and scalability beyond current usage. Minimum standards of interoperability across the stack. Fork and expansion mechanics.
