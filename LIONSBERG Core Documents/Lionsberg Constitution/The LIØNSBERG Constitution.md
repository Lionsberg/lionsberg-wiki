## How This Constitution Is Co-Written — The Year-Long Process

This Constitution is **not** the finished work of one author. The articulation below is the schematic skeleton — a starting point surfaced for the People to discern, refine, and ratify across the year-long co-writing process leading toward [[The ReFounding Phase]].

**The single-source rule.** Proposed language and ratified language never coexist in two places. The proposed articulation of each Article lives in **one place** during drafting; once ratified, it becomes the canonical text. No parallel duplication.

**The co-writing pattern that will unfold** Article by Article over the coming year — _to be activated in a later phase, not now_:

1. **The Concept** — what does this Article address?
2. **The Lineage** — how have various civilizations, traditions, and living systems handled this across human and non-human history?
3. **The LIØNSBERG Hypothesis** — given [[The Pattern Language|the Pattern]], [[The Story of LIØNSBERG|the Story]], and the cosmic-grade orientation under [[ØNE]] and [[Fractal Sovereignty]] — what is the inflection LIØNSBERG brings?
4. **The Proposed Articulation** — the actual constitutional text being proposed.
5. **The Invitation** — explicit call to the People to discern, refine, ratify (or reject and replace).
6. **The Mechanisms** — the specific structural pieces of the LIØNSBERG System that enact, embody, and operationalize the principle.

This Constitution holds a sample working text of each Article today. As the People engage each Article (and those that are missing) through the year-long process, the work happens in dialogue; refinement converges; ratification arrives; the canonical text settles. The process is paced for the year, not the day — to be activated when the Circles are gathered.

## PART I — THE RECOGNITIONS

_We acknowledge the foundational realities upon which all that follows rests. These are not opinions but recognitions of what IS._

_We recognize the [[ØNE]] — the ineffable, indivisible, ultimate Reality — as the Source of all being, soverignty, and authority. To the ØNE alone is owed the orientation of all life._

### Article I.2 — Of the Sacred Interconnection of All Things

_We recognize that all that is — all beings, all worlds, all dimensions, all moments — proceeds from the ØNE and returns to the ØNE. We are not separate. ## PART II — THE COVENANTS

_What we sacredly bind ourselves to. Entry by [[Assent]]. Always voluntary. Always revocable. Never imposed._

### Article II.1 — Of Entry by Assent

_Membership in the People of LIØNSBERG is granted not by birth, nationality, species, or planetary origin — but by sovereign Assent. To enter the Covenant is to read the Constitution, to weigh it freely, and to declare alignment by the act of [[Assent]]. The Assent is personal, revocable, and renewed by lived practice. ### Article II.2 — Of the Covenant with the ØNE

_We covenant first and forever with the [[ØNE]] — to orient our lives in [[Right Relationship]] with the Source, to seek Truth, to serve Love, to honor what is, to refuse what is False and Distorted._

_We covenant with all sovereign beings — to recognize their sovereignty as our own, to refuse domination, to practice [[Sacred Reciprocity]], to extend the Pattern of Right Relationship across every encounter, every Circle, every world._

### Article II.4 — Of the Covenant with Past and Future Generations

_We covenant with those who came before — to honor their gifts and learn from their failures. We covenant with those who come after — to leave the world more whole than we received it, to plant seeds we will not eat from, to think in terms of seven generations and seven hundred._

### Article II.5 — Of the Right to Withdraw Consent

_The Covenant is sacred but not coercive. Any sovereign being may withdraw their Assent at any time, for any reason, without penalty, without recrimination. Withdrawal is the test of voluntariness. _We collectively withdraw consent from the systems of [[kontrolle]] — the structures of domination, deception, extraction, and unfreedom that have ruled for far too long. _Children are sovereign beings from the moment of consciousness; their sovereignty is recognized fully even when their formal participation in the Covenant unfolds across years. From an early age — proposed canonical: age fifteen — youth may enter Provisional Citizenship, walking alongside the Circles in apprenticeship to the Covenant. _The fractal architecture of free being. Sovereignty at every scale. No throne but the [[ØNE]]._

_Each conscious being is sovereign in their own being — sovereign over their body, their mind, their spirit, their property, their voice, their work, their relationships, their movements, their conscience. _Communities formed by the federation of Circles are sovereign within their scope. Communities may localize, adapt, and enact this Constitution to their unique context, provided no localization may diminish the inherent sovereignty of any being, nor violate the Eternal Provisions of Part XII._

_Bioregions — territories defined by living ecological boundaries rather than by political borders — are sovereign within their scope. Federation of Communities into Bioregions follows the same voluntary, sovereignty-preserving principles as Circle-to-Community federation._

_Earth — and any inhabited planet — is sovereign within its scope, in [[Right Relationship]] with neighboring star systems, federations, and the broader cosmic order. No external power may rightly claim sovereign authority over a planet without the consent of its sovereign Beings._

_No being is more sovereign than another. The forms differ; the inherent dignity does not. Children, elders, those with disabilities, those of different species, those of different dimensional origins — all are equally sovereign. Hierarchies of being are refused._

## PART IV — RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

_The symmetric duality. No right without corresponding responsibility. Both are sacred._

### Article IV.1 — Of the Foundational Right and Responsibility

_Every being has the foundational right to live in [[Right Relationship]] with the [[ØNE]] and [[All]]. Every being has the foundational responsibility to live in Right Relationship with the ØNE and All. The right is given; the responsibility is co-created._

### Article IV.2 — Of Freedom of Thought, Speech, and Belief

_Every being is free to think, to speak, to believe — and is responsible for thinking truthfully, speaking honestly, and believing in alignment with what they have genuinely discerned. Compelled belief is no belief. Forbidden speech is tyranny._

### Article IV.3 — Of Freedom of Practice

_Every being is free to practice their spiritual, contemplative, healing, artistic, and life-affirming disciplines — and is responsible to do so without imposing on the sovereignty or freedom of others._

### Article IV.4 — Of Freedom of Assembly

_Sovereign beings are free to gather, to form Circles, to federate. They are responsible to do so in alignment with this Constitution and in the spirit of [[Right Relationship]]._

### Article IV.5 — Of the Right to Bear Arms for Defense of Life and Sovereignty

_Sovereign beings retain the right to defend life — their own, their loved ones', their community's, their world's. The corresponding responsibility is the discipline of restraint, the commitment to peaceful resolution wherever possible, and the refusal to use force as an instrument of domination._

### Article IV.6 — Of the Right to Privacy and the Sanctity of Inner Life

_The inner life is inviolable. Unreasonable surveillance, search, or seizure is prohibited. Beings have the right to privacy — over thought, body, communication, association, property — and the responsibility to use that privacy in alignment with the Covenant._

### Article IV.7 — Of the Right to Due Process and the Pursuit of Justice

_No being may be punished, deprived, or coerced without due process — fair, transparent, restorative, in [[Right Relationship]] with the principles of Part VII (Justice). Every being has the responsibility to participate in Truth-telling when called, in restorative process when needed, in collective justice work as life requires._

### Article IV.8 — Of the Right to Withdraw Consent from Crisis Powers

_Any emergency authority is strictly limited, time-bounded, and revocable. The People retain the right to withdraw any extraordinary authority that has been temporarily granted. No crisis grants permanent powers. (See also Part XI — Wilderness Provisions.)_

### Article IV.9 — Of the Right and Responsibility to Steward

_Every being has the right to access the [[Commons]] in proportion to their right relationship with it. Every being has the responsibility to leave the Commons more whole than they received it. (See Part VIII — Stewardship.)_

### Article IV.10 — Of Other Rights and Responsibilities Yet To Be Articulated

_The enumeration of rights and responsibilities herein shall not be construed to deny others retained by the People. The Constitution recognizes that the full articulation of Right Relationship is ongoing work, and that rights and responsibilities may surface through lived practice, dialogue, and OmniSpection that are not yet named here. The discernment of those is the perpetual work of the People._

## PART V — GOVERNANCE

_Consent-based authority. Wise eldership. Holofractal subsidiarity. No coercion._

### Article V.1 — Of the Source of Governing Authority

_All legitimate authority flows from voluntary consent of sovereign beings. No governance is rightful except that which proceeds from the assent of the governed and aligns with the [[ØNE]]._

### Article V.2 — Of Consent and Withdrawal of Consent

_Consent must be informed, voluntary, and ongoing. Consent may be withdrawn at any time. No governance structure may make itself permanent against the will of those it claims to govern._

### Article V.3 — Of Wise Eldership

_Authority is held by wise elders — those who have demonstrated capacity to serve the whole, who have walked the path, who have earned trust through faithfulness, who place the flourishing of [[All]] above their own. Eldership is a practice, not a title; recognized by those served, not claimed by those serving._

### Article V.4 — Of the Two-Coordinator Model

_Each Circle, Community, or Federation is coordinated by at least two roles: one held inward to the unit, one held outward to the larger whole. No single coordinator may concentrate authority. Coordination is service, not dominion._

### Article V.5 — Of Holofractal Subsidiarity

_Decisions are made at the smallest scale capable of holding them. Higher-level governance addresses only what cannot be addressed at lower scales. The People retain by default any power not explicitly delegated upward._

### Article V.6 — Of Vertical and Horizontal Architecture

_Governance is woven both vertically (across scales of subsidiarity) and horizontally (across peer Circles, Communities, and Federations). Both axes carry protections against capture and ossification._

### Article V.7 — Of the Permissible Use of System Power

_System Power — whether legal, economic, informational, or coercive — may only be used in service of the [[ØNE]] and [[All]]. Use of System Power against the People, in service of private gain, or in violation of Eternal Provisions is forbidden and self-revokes the authority of those who attempt it._

### Article V.8 — Of Recall, Petition, and the Return of Authority

_The People retain at all times the right to recall any office-holder, to petition any body, and to return any delegated authority to the People themselves. No officer or body may resist a proper recall or petition._

### Article V.9 — Of Multi-Generational Stewardship in Governance

_Every governance decision is weighed against its multi-generational consequences. The voices of children, of elders, of those not yet born, of those who came before, are present in the council._

_Capital subordinate to purpose. Regenerative. [[LUV]]. The Commons stewarded for all._

_Capital exists to serve life — never the reverse. No accumulation of capital may diminish the sovereignty of any being, the wholeness of any Community, or the regenerative capacity of any ecosystem. ### Article VI.2 — Of the Sacred Economy and [[LUV]]

_The People of LIØNSBERG transition from debt-and-fiat economics to a regenerative economy denominated in [[LUV]] (LIONSBERG Units of Value). Value is measured by contribution to the flourishing of [[All]], not by extraction from the Commons._

### Article VI.3 — Of the Integrated Delivery System

_The Program through which the New Civilization is built operates by the principles of [[Integrated Delivery]] — dissolving walls between domains, designing to The Goal, building through the Network of Commitments. (See [[The Program Delivery Guide]].)_

### Article VI.4 — Of the Commons

_Land, water, air, the radio spectrum, the cultural commons, the data commons, the genetic commons, and the knowledge commons are held collectively by all sovereign beings of the relevant scale. They may not be enclosed for private accumulation. They may be stewarded — by individuals, Circles, Communities — in [[Right Relationship]] with the whole._

_Periodic Jubilee — release of accumulated debts and concentrated capital — is a constitutional principle. The cadence of Jubilee is set by the People (proposed: every seven years for personal debts; every fifty years for structural reset, including the [[Covenant and Jubilee — December 2033]] referenced in the founding Covenant)._

### Article VI.6 — Of Contribution and Provision

_All who are willing and able to contribute are guaranteed the right to do so. All beings — contributing or not, willing or not, able or not — are guaranteed the provisions necessary for dignified life. The lowest among us is held first._

### Article VI.7 — Of Regenerativity and Resource Stewardship

_All economic activity is bounded by the regenerative capacity of the systems it touches. Extraction that exceeds regeneration is forbidden. The Commons are left more whole, not less, by our use._

## PART VII — JUSTICE

_Restorative. Reconciliatory. Regenerative. Punishment-as-domination ends here._

### Article VII.1 — Of the Source of Justice

_Justice flows from [[ØNE]], not from human power. Just law conforms to Universal Law; unjust law is no law. The People are not bound to obey what destroys life._

### Article VII.2 — Of Restorative Justice

_The aim of justice is restoration — restoration of relationship between offender, harmed, community, and the [[ØNE]]. Punishment-as-domination is refused. Where harm has been done, the work is reconciliation, repair, transformation — not retribution._

### Article VII.3 — Of the Legal System

_The legal system of LIØNSBERG is local, transparent, restorative, and accessible to all. It refuses the adversarial-extractive model. It rewards Truth-telling. It honors the harmed before the harmer's process. For serious charges, the People deliberate through Grand Jury and through panels of no fewer than three jurors drawn from the community. False accusation and bearing false witness carry their own accountability — the burden of Truth-telling falls on accuser as well as accused._

### Article VII.4 — Of Social and Economic Justice

_Where social or economic structures have caused systematic harm, the People undertake reparative work — repair to the harmed, transformation of the structure, accountability for those who profited. This is constitutional duty, not optional charity._

### Article VII.5 — Of the Dispute Resolution Architecture

_Disputes between sovereign beings, Circles, Communities, Bioregions, or Federations are addressed through a graduated process: direct dialogue → mediated dialogue → Circle-of-elders → bioregional council → planetary council. At every level, restoration is sought. Each level may decline to escalate if alternatives remain. No dispute is escalated to coercion until all restorative avenues are exhausted._

### Article VII.6 — Of the Cessation of Punitive Detention and the Proportionality Standard

_Detention is reserved for the protection of the People from imminent harm — never as punishment. The duration of any detention is bounded, transparent, regularly reviewed. Those detained retain their core sovereignty and their right to restorative process. No cruel or unusual treatment. No excessive or disproportionate response. The discipline of restoration is held against the integrity of the People as a whole._

### Article VII.7 — Of Just Compensation for Property Deprivation

_No being's lawfully-held property may be taken by any Circle, Community, Bioregion, or Federation without due process and just compensation. Where the Commons necessarily require an individual's contribution of property, the contribution is met with equitable exchange in proportion to what is given._

### Article XI.3 — Of Sanctuary

_The People establish Sanctuaries — Circles, Communities, Bioregions — where the New Pattern is lived, where those fleeing collapse find refuge, where the Wilderness is held with grace. Sanctuary is constitutional, not discretionary._

### Article XI.4 — Of Conscientious Objection

### Article XI.5 — Of Crisis and Emergency

_Any emergency authority invoked during the Wilderness is strictly limited:_

### Article XII.1 — Of the Eternal Provisions

_The following provisions of this Constitution are recognized as Eternal — not subject to amendment, not subject to suspension, not subject to derogation in any form, by any procedure:_

### Article XII.2 — Of Structural Immunity

_The Constitution contains explicit immunities against the diseases of governance:_

- _**Against power concentration:** No office, body, person, or institution may accumulate authority beyond what is delegated to it by consent. Two-coordinator model. Recallable at any time. Term-limited where appropriate._
- _**Against mission drift:** The Eternal Provisions and the published Mission are the canonical reference; any drift requires explicit constitutional amendment, not gradual reinterpretation._
- _**Against dogmatic ossification:** Truth-seeking is constitutional; received doctrine is not. Periodic [[OmniSpection]] tests every articulation against actual reality._
- _**Against external capture:** No external power, currency, ideology, or institution may capture the constitutional process. Foreign-influence transparency is constitutional._
- _**Against internal corruption:** Transparent financial flows, transparent decision processes, transparent accountability. Hidden authority is illegitimate authority._

### Article XII.3 — Of the Amendment Process

_All provisions of this Constitution other than the Eternal Provisions (Article XII.1) may be amended through the following process:_

1. _**Proposal**: Any sovereign being may propose an amendment, articulated in writing, with rationale._
2. _**Public consideration**: A minimum period (proposed: three solar seasons) during which the People consider, dialogue, and refine._
3. _**Circle deliberation**: Each Circle deliberates the proposal in its own context._
4. _**Spirit-conformance test**: Every amendment must progressively conform the Constitution closer to integrity with its Central Animating Spirit — toward greater [[Right Relationship]] with the [[ØNE]] and [[All]]. Amendments that drift from that orientation, that subordinate Spirit to expedience, or that weaken the Eternal Provisions are constitutionally void._
5. _**Ratification**: An eighty-five percent (85%) supermajority of (a) all members and (b) the Stewardship Circles of each member organization and community._
6. _**Inscription**: The amendment is inscribed; the Constitution is updated; the precedent is preserved in [[Constitutional Amendments]] and in the [[The LIØNSBERG Universe Guide|Universe Guide]] Part IV._

### Article XII.4 — Of Perpetuity, Secession, and Continuity

_This Constitution is perpetual in intent. Any Circle, Community, or Bioregion that no longer wishes to participate may secede by formal process — preserving the right of withdrawal articulated in Part II.5. Secession does not extinguish the Constitution for those who remain; the Constitution endures while any People hold the Covenant._

### Article XII.5 — Of Continuity of Authority

_If for any reason the formal structures of governance are interrupted, the constitutional authority continues to reside in the sovereign beings who are the People of LIØNSBERG. Authority cannot be lost by accident; it can only be transferred by explicit voluntary process or self-revoked by violation of Eternal Provisions._

### Article XII.6 — Of Leadership Development, Succession, and Passing the Flame

_The Constitution requires ongoing development of new stewards. No office is for life. [[Pass The Flame]] is constitutional — the deliberate transfer of stewardship to those who will carry it forward. Circles cultivate effective, ethical, and compassionate servant leaders who embody the values and principles of LIØNSBERG. Succession is anticipated, not improvised; the bench of stewards is continuously deepened so that no transition leaves the People without faithful leadership._
