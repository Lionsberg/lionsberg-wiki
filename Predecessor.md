# Predecessor

A **Predecessor** is a technical term from [[Integrated Delivery]] and the Architecture, Engineering, and Construction (AEC) industry, describing the sequencing of tasks within a complex [[Program of Action]].

A Predecessor is an [[Activity]] that must be completed before a dependent Activity can begin. Its counterpart is the [[Successor]] — an Activity that cannot begin until the current Activity is complete.

## Technical Function

In the [[LIONSBERG Integrated Delivery System]] and [[Lean]] project management, every [[Activity]] in a [[Network of Commitments]] is linked to at least one Predecessor and at least one [[Successor]]. These logical relationships form the backbone of the [[Critical Path]] toward [[The Goal]].

When thousands or millions of Activities are properly linked through their Predecessor and Successor relationships, the total logical network enables:

- Calculation of the [[Critical Path]]
- Effective [[Risk Management]]
- [[Pull Planning]] from [[Milestones]] back to present action
- Identification of [[Constraint Free]] work that is ready to execute

See [[11.57 A Logical Network of Commitments]] and [[Network of Commitments]] for the full technical framework.
