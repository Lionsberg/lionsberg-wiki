# 2.8 Choosing Your First Quest

You have crossed the threshold.  
You have learned the agreements.  
You have found your Circle.  
Now it is time to **act**.

A [[Quest]] is where Spirit takes flesh —  
Where intention becomes reality,  
And the [[New Civilization]] begins to take embodied form.

---

## What a Quest Is

A Quest is not a task on a checklist. It is a tangible act of goodwill that your [[Circle]] undertakes together — real work that makes life measurably more like [[Heaven On Earth]], right here and now.

Every Quest is both **inner** and **outer**. The outer work of transforming your community is inseparable from the inner work of transforming yourselves. A Quest that only changes the world but leaves you unchanged is incomplete. A Quest that only changes you but leaves the world untouched is unfinished.

Every Quest accomplished is a move in [[The Great Game of LIONSBERG|The Great Game]]. Every Circle completing its Quest and passing the flame advances the whole.

---

## How to Choose

Gather with your Circle. Open with [[The First Practice]]. Then discern together:

- **What need do we sense most strongly around us?** Look at your neighborhood, your community, your world. What is broken that you could help mend?

- **What fire burns within us?** What are you passionate about? What gifts do you carry that are longing to be used?

- **What can we actually complete this season?** Choose something achievable within ~90 days. Completion matters. The power of a Quest is not in the idea or the initiation, but in the faithful walking of its path.

- **What will stretch us without breaking us?** The best Quests live at the edge of your capacity — challenging enough to grow you, achievable enough to succeed.

- **What is the greatest good we can do right now with who we are and what we have?**

If there is something that needs to be done, that no one is doing, that your Circle is capable of and passionate about — *it is probably yours to do.*

---

## Start Small, Start Real, Start Now

Your first Quest does not need to be grand. It needs to be **real** and **true** — a seed of goodness you are ready to plant with your own hands.

It might be:

- Restoring a neglected patch of land
- Creating a mutual aid network for neighbors in need
- Hosting a gathering that builds genuine community
- Teaching a skill to those who need it
- Mapping the needs and gifts in your local area
- Clearing and caring for a shared natural space
- Offering tangible help to someone who is struggling

The point is not scale. The point is **beginning the rhythm of embodied action**. As your practice and your Circle mature, the depth and impact of your Quests will naturally grow with you.

---

## Making It Real

Once the discernment is clear:

- **Name the Quest.** Give it a title. Speak it aloud. This is a commitment.
- **Define the steps.** What needs to happen, in what order, by whom?
- **Set a timeframe.** Align it with the ~90-day season. When does it begin? When is it complete?
- **Track your progress.** Each week in your gathering, report honestly. What moved forward? What is stuck? What needs to change?
- **Complete it.** Follow through. The discipline of finishing what you start is one of the most powerful forces in the universe.

---

## The Spiral

Every Quest leads to another. Each one strengthens your Circle, deepens your practice, and expands your capacity. Over time, your Quests will grow — from local acts of goodwill to endeavors that reshape communities, bioregions, and beyond.

This is the upward spiral of [[The Great Game of LIONSBERG|The Great Game]]:

**Begin. Act. Complete. Reflect. Return. Begin again.**

From the simplest act of kindness to undertakings that transform worlds, the pattern is the same. And it all starts here — with the first Quest you dare to undertake.
