# PDG - Program Support and Financial Management

## 2.1.3 CEQA (California Environmental Quality Act)

The Program Manager began addressing CEQA requirements at various sites. CEQA efforts are intended to disclose and address all environmental regulatory requirements and coordinate with the local community in advance of construction. Efforts include:

- Analyzing and processing environmental documents pursuant to CEQA
- Initiating early communication with local communities
- Consideration of court challenges to environmental documents
- Undertaking required actions to implement adopted mitigation measures

The process includes Draft and Final Environmental Impact Reports (DEIR/FEIR), Notice of Preparation, public meetings, certification, and a 30-day Statute of Limitations period. Public outreach is also necessary early and often.

## 2.1.5 System-Wide Health Care Master Plan (SWMP)

A system-wide health care master planning process is recommended to coordinate all on-going health care activities within the correctional system. All relevant stakeholders should be convened to review legal, legislative, executive, community, security and health care concerns. The plan needs to consider short term, middle term and long term goals and needs.

Key questions include:

- What is the current capacity to deal with health care?
- What are current and anticipated needs for services?
- How can services be coordinated throughout the system?
- What prototype facilities should be available at all prisons?
- What technology innovations need to be coordinated?

## 2.2 Central Office Support Planning

A goal of lean project delivery is "application of production control throughout the life of the product from design to delivery." Most control should happen at the site because that is where major "flows" occur. However, some work is more efficiently processed from a central location. Tasks coordinated from central office:

- Information Technology (including BIM)
- Financial Management
- Reporting
- Prototype Control/Lessons Learned
- Facilities planning, design management and prototype development

### 2.2.1 Information Technology

IT includes not only hardware and software but how they are configured, supported and adopted throughout the enterprise. The Program provides a unique challenge -- bringing together over 50 different companies and providing technology for them to "speak" with each other across different platforms and firewalls. Program IT responsibility includes:

- Building Information Modeling (architectural/engineering rendering, ordering and clash detection, real-time estimating)
- Records Management (reports, archival documents, the Project Record)
- Financial management
- Communication (common calendaring, commitment logging, access to records and BIM)
- Collaboration processes
- Work Structuring, materials procurement, Value Stream Mapping, A3s, scheduling, budgeting

**Records Management**: A record is a document generated or received by an organization which serves as evidence of an activity. Document Control oversees the comprehensive document management system. Document Locator is the records management solution.

**Communication**: The Program uses common calendaring, commitment logging, access to records and BIM, collaboration processes, and protocols. Cloud software offers resolution for records access. Finding IT solutions is only one part -- the SLE team must collaboratively develop policies, protocols and processes.

### 2.2.2 Financial Management

Financial Management includes securing resources to finance the overall program, as well as daily management of budgets and procurements. Components include:

- **Funding Resources** -- Working with CPR and CDCR to identify, coordinate, document and secure funding. Options explored include Lease Revenue Bonds, I-Bank Program, and AB 55 PMIB Loans.
- **Contracting, Procurement and Accounting** -- Program-wide procurement under Alternative Procurement Procedures (Expedited Formal, Urgent Informal, and Sole Source).
- **Project Budgeting** -- The PM manages the entire Program budget. Baseline Program Budget developed collaboratively. Budget updates monthly showing actual committed costs. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) is a hierarchical coding system adopted by CSI numerical system.
- **Payment Processing** -- Payment Protocol ensures prompt payment. Pencil Draw/True-up process: monthly estimated payments with quarterly reconciliation against actual costs. Five standard Logistics Work Flows (A through E) for materials delivery.

### 2.2.2.5 Reporting

Regular project update and budget reports completed by Program Cost Manager. All budget updates tracked in appropriate cost control software. Reports include financial statements (monthly, quarterly, annual), JV Distribution Analysis, AR Cash Applications, Transaction Reports, Budget Verification, Liquidity reports, Financial Audits, Reconciliations, Cash Flow reports.
