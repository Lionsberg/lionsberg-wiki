# PDG - Conclusion and Prototype Control

## 4.1 Prototype Control

By the time of closeout, the robust feedback loop created between the Prototype Control group and the IPD site-specific design group should have resulted in a continuous updating of the Prototype "kit of parts" based on site-specific lessons learned in the field.

Further, specific installation techniques, safety warnings, conflicts resolved, etc. will have been communicated from the site to the Prototype Control group for inclusion as attachments to the "kit of parts." The specific combination of Prototype software packages -- Revit, Vico Systems, Horizontal Glue and others (or their equivalents) -- will enable these specific lessons learned to be associated visually with specific three-dimensional points on the BIM model, so that a subsequent site using a specific Prototype part will be able to utilize a learned installation technique, avoid a conflict or assure better safety because they will view, perhaps, notes or even short video clips associated with that three-dimensional point in the BIM model.

During the closeout phase, the [[Core Group]] will evaluate the effectiveness of this feedback loop and initiate changes needed to make it more effective and robust. The [[Core Group]] will also engage in conversations summarizing lessons learned regarding meeting of site goals.

## 4.2 Building Information Modeling

Even in traditional construction, the best builders continually update the as-built drawings to assure accuracy, week-by-week, as the project is constructed.

Using BIM files, these as-builts will be created and updated continuously, electronically. As a consequence, the evolving model will be available at any point during construction for use by end-user medical and security personnel in training prospective staff, long before the actual facility is available for that training. This use might even extend to Virtual Reality training.

The completed as-built BIM file will also be useful after construction, during operation of the facility by:

- Security / custody
- Maintenance and operations
- Capital Assets Management

## 4.3 Cost Modeling

Project closeout involves significant milestones for completion of financial records, making final payments and preparing final reports.

The [[Core Group]] will:

- Interface with Enterprise Support Financial Management
- Interface with Enterprise Support Contracts Management regarding close out and de-obligation
- Collaborate in the Final True-up Adjustment Process
- Provide a final, fully transparent accounting including:
  - Budget verification/confirmation
  - Recommendation of contracts close out
  - Invoice monitoring
  - Payments of incentives to all participating parties
  - Identifying potential excess in funding

## 4.4 Lessons Learned

### Continuing Feedback Loop

As part of the final closeout process, the [[Core Group]], including IPD Team leaders and Program Management leaders, will evaluate the effectiveness of the Continuing Lessons Learned Feedback Loop in the following areas:

- BIM feedback loop
- Effectiveness and lessons learned regarding meeting of site goals including:
  - Safety (accidents and injuries)
  - Quality (zero defects and zero punchlists)
  - Cost management
  - Schedule management
  - Change management
  - Management and mitigation of uncertainty
  - Learning
- Continuous feedback going forward from CDCR operations (medical, mental health, administration, security) informing any subsequent remodeling of this and other facilities and the planning and building of future facilities
