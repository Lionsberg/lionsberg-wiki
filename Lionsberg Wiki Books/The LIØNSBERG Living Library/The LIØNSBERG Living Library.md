## What This Is

The Sacred Codex (Volumes I–X, XII) is the bound spine — twelve canonical Volumes that hold the DNA of the [[New Civilization]].

But the Codex is not the totality of the canonical body of work. Around the spine grows a **Living Library** of substantive companion works — books and substrates that deepen specific aspects of the Way without trying to replace the Volumes themselves. The Library is alive — each entry continuously refines as more is learned and lived.

**Volume XI is the canonical index of that Library.** This page lists every work that meets the Library's inclusion criterion, with a brief description of what it carries and how it relates to the Codex spine.

The Library is broader than the Codex it indexes. The Codex is the centerpiece. The other entries are the deepening companions.

---

## The Inclusion Criterion

A work belongs in the Living Library if it is:

- **Substantive** — a meaningful body of work, not a fragment
- **Alive** — continuously developing; refined as the movement grows
- **Complementary** — deepens a specific aspect of one or more canonical Volumes without trying to replace them
- **Not already a Volume** — no double-counting with the canonical spine _(the Codex itself is listed as the first entry to honor the centerpiece, not to add a Volume to itself)_

Works that do not meet this — preserved as historical [[Procession]], in their own wiki book folders, accessible through the wider wiki but not in this Library.

## How the Library Grows

The Library is **continuously improving** in three senses:

- **Each entry refines** — every work above is itself a living document; it sharpens as it is lived
- **New entries can be added** — when a substantive companion work matures to canonical-companion status, it joins the Library. The inclusion criterion is the gate.
- **Entries can be retired** — if a work's content is fully metabolized into a Volume, or if it ceases to be alive, it can be removed from the Library and preserved as procession

Additions and retirements pass through the [[OmniSpection]] discipline that governs the Codex.
