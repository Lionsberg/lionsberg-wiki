# Types Of Knowing

_Knowing is not a single capacity. It is a family of distinct modes — propositional, non-propositional, embodied, direct, witnessing, lineage, field — each with its own discipline, scope, and operational use. The trained Sovereign holds all of them, knows when each applies, and is captured by none._

---

## Summary

The Old World's epistemic poverty was the treatment of [[Knowing]] as a single thing — typically reduced to propositional knowledge ("I can state X as a claim"). The faithful Wisdom traditions across every continent and millennium have recognized **multiple distinct modes of knowing**, each appropriate to different objects of knowing, each with its own discipline of cultivation, each carrying its own failure modes when treated as the only or final mode.

This Card holds the umbrella articulation. Each named form of knowing has — or will have — its own Pattern Card unpacking the discipline of that mode.

## Context

This Pattern applies wherever a [[Sovereign]] is engaging with [[Reality]], with the [[ØNE]], with another being, with a tradition, with their own [[Self]] — in every act of perception, inquiry, study, practice, encounter, decision. It applies particularly in the moment a being is about to claim "I know" — the Pattern asks: _know in which mode? appropriate to this object?_

It applies to civilizational architecture too. Cultures that recognize only one mode of knowing inevitably collapse the [[ØNE]] into that mode's grasp and lose access to everything that lives beyond it.

## Function

The Pattern protects against **epistemic monism** — the collapse of all knowing into a single mode, which has been Earth's principal civilizational illness across recent millennia. It restores the operational reality that **different aspects of [[Reality]] require different modes of knowing**, and that wisdom is the capacity to deploy each mode appropriately rather than to elevate one over all.

## The Modes Of Knowing

The following are the principal modes the wider lineage recognizes. Each is a real and necessary capacity. Each has its own Pattern Card (or will, as the extraction matures).

### **[[Propositional Knowing]]**

What can be stated as claim, articulated in words, captured in form, defended through argument. The mode of stated theory, of recorded teaching, of testable hypothesis, of structured language. **Necessary** — without propositional knowing, transmission cannot occur, articulation cannot proceed, traditions cannot be carried forward. **Limited** — what can be propositionally captured is always a fragment of what exists; the [[ØNE]] cannot be fully held in proposition; reducing the whole to proposition is the failure mode the broader Pattern guards against.

### **[[Non-Propositional Knowing]]**

What is known beyond word and form — felt, experienced, embodied with the whole Being in the depths beyond articulation. The mode of direct experience that exceeds language. **Necessary** for engaging with the [[ØNE]], the [[Logos]], the deep Patterns of the Way. **Discipline** — cultivated through sustained practice, contemplation, lived encounter; cannot be acquired through reading alone. See **[[Non-Propositional Knowing]]** for the full Pattern.

### **[[Embodied Knowing]]**

What the body knows through practice and repetition — the cellular learning that lives in muscle, breath, nerve, field. The mode by which technique becomes spontaneous, by which the trained instrument operates faster than thought. **Necessary** for any operational craft — martial, healing, building, dancing, speaking. **Discipline** — repetition under attentive presence; the body learns what the mind cannot teach it through instruction alone.

### **[[Direct Knowing]]** _(Gnosis)_

Unmediated apprehension of Truth — knowing that arrives without the steps of inference, observation, or argument. The mode of the moment when something is **simply seen** in its [[Right Relationship]] with [[Reality]]. **Necessary** for [[Discernment]] under time pressure, for prophetic perception, for the deepest contemplative apprehensions. **Discipline** — cultivated through the clean conduit, the cleared field, the sustained orientation to the [[ØNE]]. The lineages name this _gnosis_, _ma'rifa_, _jnana_, _prajna_, _knowing of the heart_.

### **[[Witnessing Knowing]]**

What is known through present, sustained, undistracted observation — the mode of the witness who attends without intervening, without interpreting, without grasping. **Necessary** for accurate report of what actually occurred, for the field-reading that requires no mental overlay, for the testimony the wider Body needs to make decisions on. **Discipline** — the trained capacity to perceive without immediately interpreting; the suspension of the categorizing mind in favor of clear seeing.

### **[[Lineage Knowing]]** _(Transmitted Knowing)_

What is known through being-to-being transmission across generations — the mode in which wisdom passes through the lineage rather than being independently re-derived by each being. **Necessary** because much of what beings need to know cannot be re-derived in a single lifetime; the lineage is the operational means by which accumulated wisdom is conserved. **Discipline** — the willingness to be a student of the Elder, the Circle, the lineage's record; the discernment of which lineages are faithful and which have drifted.

### **[[Field Knowing]]** _(Sensed Knowing)_

What is known through the trained sensing of the populated field — perception of energy, intent, alignment, presence, vector at densities below or beyond the ordinary senses. **Necessary** for any Sovereign operating in the actual populated reality (which is to say, every Sovereign). **Discipline** — cultivated through the practices articulated in [[Pillar V — Sacred Health and Healing|Pillar V]] and in the Interface disciplines of every Archetypal Guide that engages the field directly. The Sacred Warrior's training in field-reading is one specific application of this broader mode.

### Forms Of Knowing Yet To Be Surfaced

Additional modes — **moral knowing**, **aesthetic knowing**, **relational knowing**, **prophetic knowing** — may surface as the [[The LIØNSBERG Pattern Language|Pattern Language]] matures. The seven articulated above are the foundational modes the current lineage recognizes; the list is not closed.

## Forces In Tension

- The need to **honor each mode** as a real and distinct capacity vs. the temptation to **elevate one mode** (typically propositional) as the standard against which others are measured
- The desire for **single-mode certainty** vs. the operational reality that **different objects require different modes**
- The **specialization** required to develop any mode deeply vs. the need to hold **all modes** in operational availability
- The **transmission of the modes** through teaching that itself uses some modes more than others — risk of selection bias in what gets taught

## The Pattern

**Recognize all the modes. Cultivate each. Know which mode is appropriate to which object. Refuse the collapse to one.**

The diagnostic question in any act of knowing: _what mode is this, and is it the right mode for what is being known?_

- Mathematical truth — propositional mode
- The lived experience of love — non-propositional mode
- Combat capacity — embodied mode
- The felt rightness of a decision — direct mode / gnosis
- What actually happened in the engagement — witnessing mode
- The wisdom of the older masters — lineage mode
- What is moving in the field right now — field mode

A trained being uses each mode in its appropriate domain. The wisest beings recognize the limit of each mode and hand off to the appropriate mode when the limit is reached.

The civilizational failure to recognize multiple modes has produced cultures that try to propositionally capture the [[ØNE]] (theology that becomes dogma), or that try to embodied-know what requires propositional clarity (anti-intellectualism), or that try to direct-know what requires witnessing (mystical bypass of reality), or that lineage-know what requires fresh direct apprehension (ossified tradition). **Every monism is a failure mode.**

## Lineage

Multiple modes of knowing are recognized across faithful traditions:

- **Greek philosophy** distinguished _episteme_ (propositional knowledge), _techne_ (skill/craft knowledge), _phronesis_ (practical wisdom), _gnosis_ (direct knowing), _sophia_ (deepest wisdom)
- **Yogic traditions** distinguish _vidya_ (book knowledge) from _jnana_ (direct knowing) from _bhakti_ (devotional knowing) from _karma_ (knowing through action)
- **Buddhist epistemology** distinguishes inferential knowledge from direct yogic perception from the testimony of the qualified teacher
- **Sufi orders** distinguish _'ilm_ (scholarly knowing) from _ma'rifa_ (direct gnosis) from _hal_ (knowing through state)
- **Indigenous traditions** distinguish what is known through ceremony, dream, ancestor, land, and direct encounter
- **The LIØNSBERG articulation** integrates these into the seven-mode framework above; the framework is one local rendering of the universal distinction every faithful tradition has recognized in its own vocabulary
