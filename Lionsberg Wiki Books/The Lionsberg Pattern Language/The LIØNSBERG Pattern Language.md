## Integrated Program Delivery (IPD)

_How the Patterns articulated across the Twelve Pillars are actually delivered, at scale, with reliability. Once the substantive Patterns are known, the work of integrating them into operational programs and projects becomes its own discipline — the methodology by which complex multi-party production becomes coherent. These integration and project-management Patterns are drawn from $20M+ of funded research in [[The Program Delivery Guide|the IPD/AEC Program Delivery Guide]] — proven across trillions of dollars of economy — and form the operational layer that ties the Pillar-specific Patterns together into delivered Reality. Specific Pattern Cards to be articulated as the canonical Pattern Language rebuild proceeds._

### Contractual and Delivery Framework

- [[Integrated Project Delivery]] — unified team where owner, designers, builders, and trade partners collaborate from planning through delivery
- [[Target Value Design]] — design process where target cost is a primary design criterion alongside quality and schedule
- [[Last Planner System]] — backward-from-milestone commitment-based planning with constraint identification and reliable promises
- [[Single Lean Enterprise]] — organizational structure unifying multiple companies' best resources under shared governance for program success

### Collaborative Governance

- [[Core Group]] — 4-6 equal-stature leaders from key organizations making consensus-based decisions; inverts command-control
- [[Community of Practice]] — peer-led learning group cutting across organizational boundaries to share knowledge and improve processes
- [[Network of Commitments]] — reliability through clear requests, explicit commitments, and tracking variance (also Core Pattern Element 11)

### Design and Value

- [[Kit of Parts]] — standardized modular components recombined flexibly for different contexts without re-designing the whole
- [[Set-Based Design]] — defer final choices until the right combination of pre-analyzed alternatives emerges to simultaneously meet multiple constraints
- [[Work Structuring]] — systematic investigation of the entire production system (product + process + supply chain) to create predictable, reliable workflow

### Production and Planning

- [[Pull Planning]] — schedule work backward from milestone dates; team makes clear requests and realistic commitments with defined handoff expectations
- [[Constraint Analysis and Removal]] — weekly look-ahead to identify blocking conditions and remove them before work enters execution
- [[Value Stream Mapping]] — map current-state process steps, eliminate non-value-adding activities, model optimal flow aligned to customer demand
- [[Strategic Sourcing]] — five-phase methodology to identify, qualify, and contract with optimal trade partners for total responsible cost

### Quality and Safety

- [[Built-In Quality]] — weave quality expectations into design and processes; empower workers to stop production if handoff criteria aren't met
- [[First Run Studies]] — test design on first instance of operation; establish conditions of satisfaction; prove simplification before full rollout
- [[Error Proofing]] — design processes and spaces to prevent errors rather than catch them after; basis for zero-defects culture
- [[Safety-In-Design]] — integrate safety into construction documents and daily plans as a primary design criterion, not an afterthought
- [[ROY System]] — standardized Red/Orange/Yellow early-detection notification to flag quality/safety issues, fix immediately, notify stakeholders

### Learning and Continuous Improvement

- [[Plan-Do-Check-Act]] — PDCA cycle embedded in all processes; regular assessment of what works, what doesn't, and how to improve
- [[Lessons Learned Capture and Dissemination]] — systematic documentation and sharing of improvements across all scales (zone, area, trade, program) via multiple channels
- [[A3 Reports]] — structured one-page problem-solving format (background, problem, current/future state, countermeasures) using the scientific method
- [[Five-Why Analysis]] — root-cause investigation to move beyond symptoms; embedded in plan failure review and incident investigations

### Cost Management

- [[Real-Time Estimating]] — continuous cost extraction and updates as design changes; enables set-based design and TVD tradeoffs
- [[Collective Risk Management]] — entire team works together to minimize overall project risk; risk shared across parties rather than shifted
- [[Financial Incentives Plan]] — align stakeholder interests by rewarding behaviors that serve project goals (quality, safety, innovation, cost reduction)

### Execution and Materials

- [[Standard Work]] — documented baseline/best-practice processes; provides stable platform for continuous improvement; output of First Run Studies
- [[Vendor Managed Inventory]] — shared store where multiple trades pool demand and source consumables; min/max quantities reduce waste and variability
- [[Just-In-Time Delivery]] — material delivered when needed, where needed; minimizes time on-site and intermediate handling

### Leadership and Culture

- [[Retrospectives]] — regularly scheduled facilitated sessions where team identifies what works, what doesn't, what to change; ensures all viewpoints heard
- [[Advantage-Based Decision Making]] — structure decisions on importance of advantages; record rationale for future reference (also called Choosing By Advantages)
