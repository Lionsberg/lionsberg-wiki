# The Test of Belonging

A clean operational criterion for what to include and what to exclude.

> **If it belongs in the New World, it belongs in LIØNSBERG.**
> **If it does not belong in the New World, it does not belong in LIØNSBERG.**

The criterion is binary and recursive. Every practice, every tool, every term, every institution, every relationship, every Pattern can be tested against it.

- Does this belong in [[Heaven On Earth]]? Then it belongs in the Codex, in the Playbook, in the Pattern Language, in the Circle's life.
- Does this belong in the Old World pattern of kontrolle, extraction, separation, and forgetting? Then it does not belong in LIØNSBERG, regardless of how familiar, comfortable, or culturally embedded it has become.

The Test of Belonging cuts both ways. It welcomes what is good — even when its provenance is unusual. It refuses what is anti-good — even when its provenance is familiar. It is **agnostic to origin** and **clear about destination**.

Applications:

- A technology that surveils beings against their consent: does not belong.
- A practice that releases stored trauma and restores sovereignty: belongs.
- A money system that creates artificial scarcity: does not belong.
- A money system that flows value to the work that serves life: belongs.
- A religion that places intermediaries between beings and Source: does not belong (in the religion-as-system sense).
- A religion's deepest truths that point to direct communion with the Sacred: belong (folded forward).

The Test of Belonging is what allows the New Civilization to **harvest from every tradition** while remaining itself. It is also what protects the New Civilization from inheriting Old World patterns by accident.

It pairs with [[The Filling and Removing Dialectic]]: the Test of Belonging tells you **what** to fill or remove; the Dialectic is **how** to fill or remove.

Lives canonically in [[The LIØNSBERG Playbook]] as the operational discernment criterion.
