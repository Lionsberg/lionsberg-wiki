# Federations vs Empires

The civilizational design axis. Across the Cosmos, every civilization that has ever held together has been either:

**A Federation** — voluntary association of sovereign Creatures, held together by Wisdom, Love, and Influence. Aligned with the Way. **Good.**

**An Empire** — coerced association held together by Force. Misaligned with the Way. **Anti-Good.**

There are no third options at scale. Civilizations that try to be both collapse — usually into Empire, occasionally back into Federation.

**The Federation pattern produces:**
- Flourishing across diversity
- Resilience under stress
- Multi-generational thriving
- Right Relationship across species and worlds
- Compressed power ratios
- Voluntary continuation

**The Empire pattern produces:**
- Concentration of power
- Hierarchy under Force
- Brittleness at the periphery
- Exhaustion of the source
- The predator-prey dynamic that breeds the Dark Fleet
- Forced continuation requiring ever more Force

Earth is at the **end** of an Empire epoch and the **beginning** of a Federation epoch. The Turn is the choice between continuing the Empire pattern at higher tech, or instantiating the Federation pattern at planetary scale and then beyond.

This axis is the spine of [[The InterCosmic Treaty of Non-Subjugation]] — Empires violate the Treaty by their very nature; Federations honor it by their very nature.

Cosmic history is largely the record of Federations and Empires across worlds. Most worlds eventually choose. The choice is binding for generations.
