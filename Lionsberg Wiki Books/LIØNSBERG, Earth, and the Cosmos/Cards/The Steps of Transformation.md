# The Steps of Transformation

The operational sequence that takes a Circle from intention to outcome.

This is the working methodology — distinct from the [[The Seven Moves|Seven Moves]] rhythm, which is the cyclical practice. The Steps of Transformation are the **deeper structural sequence** that any Quest moves through, from first articulation to measured impact:

- **Story** — name what is happening. Articulate the situation truthfully, in living language, so the Circle (and those it serves) can see clearly.
- **Vision and Design** — articulate what is to be co-created. Specify the outcome in enough detail that the work can be planned and the result can be recognized when it arrives.
- **Context and Analysis** — understand the field. Who is affected. What forces are at play. What constraints are operating. What allies are present. What obstacles are real.
- **Engagement** — bring in those whose participation is needed and welcomed. Pull, not push. Resonance, not recruitment.
- **Strategy and Planning** — translate vision into operational sequence. Milestones. Commitments. The Network of Commitments through which the Circle will deliver.
- **Action** — do the work. Keep promises. Adjust when reality teaches. The bulk of the time.
- **Measurement, Retrospection, and Improvement** — see what actually happened. Compare to the vision. Learn what worked and what did not. Refine the Pattern for next time.

These Steps are **fractal** — they operate at the scale of a single conversation, a 90-day Quest, a year-long initiative, a decade of civilizational work. The same sequence, scaled.

They pair with the [[Seven Moves]] in this way: the Seven Moves are the **rhythm of the Circle** (forming, weaving, choosing, doing, celebrating, sharing, passing); the Steps of Transformation are the **architecture of the work** itself. Together they describe how the Pattern enacts itself in time.
