# The Filling and Removing Dialectic

The paired Co-Creation moves of the New Civilization.

**Filling Up What Is Lacking** — finding what is missing in the world and co-creating it. Schools where there are none. Healing where there is sickness. Story where there is silence. Bread where there is hunger. Practice where there is theory. **Building** rather than complaining.

**Removing What Does Not Belong** — finding what is wrong in the world and withdrawing it. The Old World pattern. The predatory practice. The extractive structure. The lie that masquerades as truth. [[Hospicing the Old Systems|Hospicing what is dying]]. Calling out what cannot be carried forward.

The two moves are paired. **Either alone is incomplete.**

**Pure Filling without Removing** builds the New on top of the Old. The Old grows back through the new structures. The poison persists in the soil.

**Pure Removing without Filling** tears down the Old and leaves a vacuum. The void invites worse than what was removed. The cleared ground grows what blew in on the wind.

**Together** — _fill what is lacking; remove what does not belong; replace what is failing; improve what can be made better_ — they constitute the operational dialectic of every Circle, every Community, every Federation.

The dialectic answers the daily question of [[The LIØNSBERG Playbook|every Co-Creator]]: **What is mine to fill today? What is mine to remove?**
