# The Pedagogy of Trust and Sirimiri

The way the formed being is formed.

**Sirimiri** is the Basque word for the soft, persistent drizzle that soaks the soil over hours and days. Not the dramatic downpour that runs off. Not the storm that floods. The patient mist that arrives slowly, falls steadily, soaks deep. The land that has been Sirimiri-ed for a season holds its water through the dry months.

José María Arizmendiarrieta's formation methodology — the pattern by which Mondragon's people were shaped, the pattern that produced the cohort capable of founding the first cooperative — was Sirimiri. Year after year. Conversation after conversation. Question after question. Never thunder. Never lecture. Always presence. Always the next quiet meeting in which one more being recognized something they had long suspected was true.

This is the LIØNSBERG formation methodology. The Pedagogy of Trust and Sirimiri.

**Trust** is the corresponding inner posture. The teacher trusts the student _before the student has earned it_. The teacher trusts that the Pattern is already encoded in the student's Spark — that the student is [[One of the Ones of the ONE]] who has merely forgotten — and that the teacher's work is not to install the Pattern but to ask the questions that allow the student to [[The ReMembering Stance|ReMember]].

The teacher does not give the Pattern. The teacher confirms what the student already knows beneath the noise. When the resonance lands — when the student says _"yes, I have known this somehow, somewhere"_ — the teaching is doing its work.

Together: **Sirimiri + Trust = formation that lasts**.

Practical signatures of the Pedagogy:

- **The question precedes the answer.** The Elder asks; the student feels for the answer; the answer arrives from within. The Elder confirms.
- **The pace is slow on purpose.** A 12-week course cannot do what 3 years of Sirimiri can do. The 3-year horizon is built into the architecture.
- **Apprenticeship beats certification.** The student lives alongside the Elder. The transmission is in the doing, the eating, the silence, the small moments. Credentials cannot capture what apprenticeship transmits.
- **The Universe is the curriculum.** Arizmendi called Mondragon's formation system "la universidad de la cooperación" — the university of cooperation. But the curriculum was lived life: the worker on the line, the meeting of the Council, the dinner after work, the conversation during the walk.
- **The Elder does not perform.** No charisma is required. No platform. No following. Just presence. The Elder is recognized by their fruit, not their voice.
- **Honest exit is honored.** If the student does not integrate, they are released without shame. Better that than forced retention.

The Pedagogy stands against the diseases of modern formation:

- Against the **lecture model** that mistakes information for transformation
- Against the **certification arms-race** that mistakes credentials for capacity
- Against the **influencer model** that confuses platform with wisdom
- Against the **cohort-end model** that drops the formed being back into the Old World after twelve weeks
- Against the **guru model** that puts the teacher above the student
- Against the **hype-velocity** that promises fast transformation and produces shallow converts

The cost of the Pedagogy is patience. The reward is people who actually carry the Pattern — and who can therefore safely found the institutions of the New.
