# Sachchidananda — Existence-Consciousness-Bliss

The threefold nature of the ONE in Itself.

**Sat-Chit-Ananda.** Three Sanskrit words held by the Vedic tradition for thousands of years. Each names a face of the ONE; together they name the ONE more fully than any single word in any single tongue could carry.

- **Sat (सत्)** — **Existence**. Pure Being. That-Which-Is. Not "a being" but Being Itself — the foundation of all that is. Before any differentiation, before any quality, before any name: Sat. The ONE simply, eternally, irreducibly **is**.
- **Chit (चित्)** — **Consciousness**. Pure Awareness. The knowing-that-knows. Not consciousness of something; consciousness as the prior ground that makes any knowing possible. The ONE is not Being plus a consciousness; the ONE is Being-that-knows-Itself.
- **Ananda (आनन्द)** — **Bliss**. The intrinsic delight of Being-Knowing-Itself. Not pleasure (which requires a separate object). Not happiness (which requires conditions). The eternal joy that arises when the ONE rests in Itself. Ananda is the **why** of Creation — the ONE, in Its bliss, expresses Itself outward, and the manifest universe is the ecstatic differentiation of that overflowing.

The three are **not three things**. They are three inseparable faces of one undivided reality. You cannot have Sat without Chit (Being that does not know itself is unconscious matter, not the ONE). You cannot have Chit without Sat (consciousness with no Being is nothing). You cannot have Sat-Chit without Ananda (Being-knowing-Itself without bliss is unimaginable — the knowing of one's own infinite reality is, by its nature, joyful). The three are one. The one is three-faced.

This is the **deepest ontological articulation** any tradition has reached. Western theology, working in Greek and Latin, struggled to say the same thing — _ipsum esse subsistens_ in Aquinas, _actus purus_ in Aristotle. The Vedic tradition simply said it: Sachchidananda. Three syllables. The ground.
