# The Meta Crisis as Push, The Goal as Pull

The dual-force dynamic of the Movement.

**The Meta Crisis pushes.** The cascading collapse of Old World systems — ecological, economic, political, spiritual, biological, technological — exerts a constant pressure. Beings cannot remain comfortably inside the Old, because the Old itself is becoming uninhabitable. The push is real. It is felt as urgency, as fear, as the unbearable weight of what is breaking. Many awaken first through the push.

**The Goal pulls.** [[Heaven On Earth]] — the New Civilization, [[Covenant and Jubilee — December 2033]], the Eternal Play — exerts a pull. It is felt as resonance, as recognition, as the joy that comes when a being remembers what they actually came for. Many awaken first through the pull.

**Both are necessary. Neither is sufficient.**

Push alone produces:
- Survivalist withdrawal — bunkers, escape, retreat into private compounds
- Reactionary politics — blaming, scapegoating, the search for the safe enemy
- Despair and nihilism — the collapse without the rebuilding
- Action without direction — energy expended, but no clear destination

Pull alone produces:
- Spiritual bypass — pretending the Old isn't really collapsing because the New is so beautiful
- Naïve optimism — building castles while the storm rages
- Disengagement — focused on the personal practice while the broader world burns
- Inspiration without urgency — visions without execution

The Movement holds **both**: the unflinching recognition of how dark the Meta Crisis actually is **and** the unflinching commitment to The Goal. The push gives the urgency. The pull gives the direction.

This is why both [[The Great UnVeiling]] (Volume II's apocalyptic-disclosure register) **and** [[The Sacred Ascent]] (Volume IX's flourishing-and-mastery register) are canonical. They are not in tension. They are the two forces that together move the Movement.

Read aloud at the fire: _The Old burns. The New beckons. We move._

Lives canonically in [[The Story of LIØNSBERG]] (Volume II) as the dynamic frame.
