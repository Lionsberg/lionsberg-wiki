# The Network of Commitments

The heartbeat of integrated delivery. The pulse of a New Civilization at work.

A promise that is not made cannot be kept. A commitment that is not visible cannot be supported. The Old World ran on schedules — top-down, push-based, unreliable. The New runs on commitments — networked, pull-based, accountable to the people in the room.

The cycle has four states:

- **SHOULD** — what the larger Pattern wants from this work. The Goal cascades downward. Every Circle inherits a SHOULD from the Hub. Every Hub inherits a SHOULD from the Pillar. Every Pillar inherits its SHOULD from The Goal itself.
- **CAN** — what the resources, predecessors, and conditions actually permit. The Circle examines reality. "Given what we have, what is possible?" Not what is asked. Not what is dreamed. What is _possible_.
- **WILL** — the commitment the team makes. This is the moment of integrity. The Circle promises a thing they have weighed against the CAN. The promise is recorded; the team becomes accountable to itself and to its neighbors.
- **DID** — what actually happened. Measured against the WILL. The gap is the learning signal. **Percent Promises Kept (PPK)** is the diagnostic — climb it from 50% to 80%+ and reliability transforms.

The four states stack across the entire Movement. Each Circle's WILL becomes another Circle's CAN. Each Hub's commitments become the predecessor for the bioregion's. The whole Movement forms a **Network** — every node holding promises that other nodes depend on.

The Network is **pull-based**, not push-based. Downstream work pulls from upstream commitments. No one is given a schedule and told to comply; everyone makes a commitment and the schedule emerges from those commitments. This is the Last Planner System (Glenn Ballard) applied to civilization.

Three disciplines hold the Network together:

- **Constraint cycling** — before committing, the team identifies what would prevent the commitment. The constraints are made visible. The team works the constraints, not the symptoms.
- **First Run Studies** — for new work, the first iteration is treated as a learning experiment. The team observes, refines, then ramps. Reliability lifts before scale arrives.
- **Retrospect-and-Adjust** — every cycle examines what worked and what did not. The system that learns fastest, wins. "Every defect is a gift."

The Network is also a **trust technology**. Where there is no trust, commitments are empty performance. Where there is trust, commitments compose into reliability across thousands of nodes. The 9-day cycle time of the Movement, the Fibonacci spread, the bioregional grid — all rest on this Network.
