# The Terms of the New Covenant

The substantive content of the sacred agreement.

[[The Covenant Stack]] names the architecture. The Terms name what the architecture actually binds. The New Covenant is not a vague spiritual gesture; it has specific Terms — known, agreed, recorded — that anyone joining the Pattern is consenting to live by.

The Terms are a seven-fold spine. Each Term names a face of right relationship that has been violated by the Old World and is now being restored:

**Truth.** No falsehood in the inner room of the Self. No lie in the speaking of the Self. No deception in the dealings with others. The Pilgrim under Covenant tells the truth — to themselves first, to their Circle next, to the world thereafter. Where truth cannot yet be told publicly, it is told privately; the Pilgrim does not collaborate with the lie even by silence.

**Love.** The orienting frequency of every act. Not sentimental love. Not transactional love. Agapē — the willed-good-of-the-other regardless of return. The Term binds: every move the Pilgrim makes is made under the standard of Love. Where Love and another value appear to conflict, the Pilgrim discerns more deeply; Love is the deeper truth that resolves apparent contradictions.

**Justice.** Right relationship made operational. The Pilgrim does not extract. The Pilgrim does not exploit. The Pilgrim does not collaborate with extractive systems beyond what survival in the transition requires — and the Pilgrim minimizes that. Justice under the Covenant is **restorative**, not retributive — but restorative justice still names the violation, still requires the restoration, still protects the harmed.

**Stewardship.** Nothing is owned. Everything is held in trust. The Earth, the body, the gift, the resource, the relationship — all are loaned to the Pilgrim by ONE for the term of the Pilgrim's incarnation. The Pilgrim leaves what they touch better than they found it, or dies trying.

**Wisdom.** The Pilgrim seeks counsel. The Pilgrim respects elders. The Pilgrim does not act in haste against the Discernment of their Circle. The Pilgrim is teachable, even when ranked high; even the Master is a student of the Way. Wisdom under the Covenant requires humility — the recognition that no Pilgrim has the whole, and that the whole emerges through the gathering.

**Unity.** The Pilgrim chooses Higher Order Functional Unity over factional victory. The Pilgrim does not break the body of the Movement to win the argument. Where the Pilgrim has dissent, it is given through the channels of [[Honoring Dissent]] — not by sabotage, not by exit-without-process, not by external broadcast of internal grievance. Unity does not require agreement; it requires not weaponizing disagreement.

**Liberty / Beauty / Goodness** (the seventh as a triadic completion). The Pilgrim's life expresses Liberty — the sovereignty that the Pattern protects at every scale. The Pilgrim's work expresses Beauty — not aesthetic decoration but the inner geometry of right-relationship made visible. The Pilgrim's character expresses Goodness — the integration of all the other Terms into the actual texture of how the Pilgrim moves through the world.

The Terms are **mutually reinforcing**. Each guards the others. Truth without Love becomes cruelty. Love without Truth becomes complicity. Justice without Mercy becomes vengeance. Stewardship without Wisdom becomes neurosis. Unity without Liberty becomes empire. Liberty without Unity becomes fragmentation. Beauty without Goodness becomes aestheticism. Goodness without Beauty becomes drudgery. The seven hold each other.

The Terms also have **antecedents**. Every great covenantal tradition gestured at them. The Hebrew Decalogue. The Sermon on the Mount. The Eightfold Path. The Five Pillars. The Yamas and Niyamas. The seven-fold Christian virtues. None of these were complete on their own; together they reveal the same underlying Terms. The Pattern does not invent the Terms; the Pattern **collects them**, refines them, integrates them, names them for this hour.

Acceptance of the Covenant is **voluntary**. No one is forced to enter. The Pilgrim crosses the Threshold of their own consent. But once entered, the Terms apply — and the Circle holds the Pilgrim accountable to what the Pilgrim themselves chose. This is not authoritarian; this is integrity. The Pilgrim who breaks the Terms is not punished; the Pilgrim who breaks the Terms is held by the Circle in conversation about whether they wish to remain Pilgrim, or whether the Way is not theirs to walk.

The Terms are the **substantive content** that [[The Covenant Stack]] structures — Personal Covenant, Circle Covenant, Hub Covenant, Bioregional Covenant, Grid Covenant, Universal Covenant. At every scale, the same Terms apply; only the parties and the specifics differ. This is [[Holofractal Omnifederation]] expressed as covenant: the same agreements at every scale.

Lives canonically in [[The LIØNSBERG Pattern Language]] (Volume III) as the substantive Element 3 (Field of Agreements), and in [[The LIØNSBERG Lexicon]] (Volume VII) as the Meta Layer articulation of the Covenant's content.
