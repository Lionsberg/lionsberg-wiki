# Hospicing the Old Systems

The New Civilization does not destroy the Old. It **hospices** it.

The Old World cannot be toppled by force without becoming what it overthrew. It cannot be reformed from within without being co-opted by what it contains. It can only be **allowed to die well** while the New is built alongside.

To hospice the Old means:

- **Recognize** that the Old is dying — by its own pathologies, not by our hand.
- **Refuse to feed it** — withdraw consent, attention, money, energy, time. This is the operational form of [[Withdrawal of Consent and Exodus Protocols]] at the system level.
- **Refuse to fight it on its terms** — that fight strengthens it.
- **Build the New alongside it** — so beings have somewhere to go as the Old collapses.
- **Honor what was true in the Old** — every dying civilization carries some truth worth preserving for the next.
- **Receive those leaving the Old** — without humiliation, without test, without cost. The exit must be wide and gentle.
- **Compost what remains** — let the matter return to the field; the form is what dies, not the substance.
- **Mourn cleanly** — the Old produced beauty along with horror. Both are real. Both deserve their acknowledgment.

Hospicing is the operational complement to [[Withdrawal of Consent and Exodus Protocols]]:
- **Withdrawal** is what an individual or Circle does — disengage from the structures that violate the Pattern.
- **Hospicing** is what the wider Movement does for the larger systems — receive the dying, build the new, ease the transition for all.

**The Old World cannot be destroyed by force. It is held together by consent. When enough consent is withdrawn, it dissolves of its own weight.** The Movement's task is to ensure something better is already standing when that happens — and that no being is left without a place to go.
