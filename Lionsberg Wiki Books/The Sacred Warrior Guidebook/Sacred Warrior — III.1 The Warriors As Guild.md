# Sacred Warrior — III.1 The Warriors As Guild

_The Warriors as one Guild of the LIØNSBERG Body. The Guild's internal architecture. How a Warrior joins. How the Guild governs itself. The protections against capture, drift, and corruption._

---

## What A Guild Is In LIØNSBERG

A [[Guild]] in LIØNSBERG is a voluntary association of those who practice a shared craft or carry a shared calling. Guilds operate **horizontally** across every [[Levels of Scale|scale]] — from individual to planetary to cosmic — each holding its specific functional contribution to the [[ØNE Body|LIØNSBERG Body]].

The Warriors are one Guild among approximately twelve archetypal Guilds corresponding to each of [[The 12 Pillars of LIONSBERG|the Twelve Pillars]]. The Healers Guild. The Teachers Guild. The Builders Guild. The Stewards Guild. The Storytellers Guild. The Midwives Guild. The Witnesses Guild. The Guardians Guild. The Cosmic Diplomats Guild. The Cooks Guild. The Architects Guild. The Warriors Guild.

Each Guild is **sovereign within its craft**. The Warriors do not tell the Healers how to heal. The Healers do not tell the Warriors when to engage. Each Guild holds its specific function with its own training, its own lineage, its own protocols, its own Wisdom Council. Each is accountable to the wider Body for the contribution of its craft, and federates with the other Guilds in [[Right Relationship]].

## The Warrior Guild's Functional Position

Within the [[ØNE Body]], the Warriors hold the **active defense, clearing, and projective-force capacities** — the body's capacity to protect itself, to clear what does not belong, to project force when required by the field's restoration. This function is necessary in any civilization that operates in a populated cosmos where some factions do not respect [[Right Relationship]]. The function has been carried under various names across every world that has been formed enough to need it.

The Warriors are distinguished from the other Guilds by:

- **The depth of training in the energetic-martial-spiritual disciplines** that this guidebook articulates
- **The Vow that binds the cultivated capacity** to service of [[ØNE]] and [[All]] — a Vow of particular gravity because of the destructive capacity the training produces
- **The protocols of engagement** that govern how the Guild responds when engagement is required
- **The accountability structures** that hold the Guild against the failure modes its capacities are particularly susceptible to
- **The home Pillar of [[Pillar X — Sacred Guardianship and Order]]** — the substantive domain the Warrior Guild federates into

The Warriors are not the only Guild with protective function. The [[Pillar X — Sacred Guardianship and Order|Guardians]] Guild specifically holds the broader guardianship work, often without the same emphasis on combat-trained capacity. The Healers, the Stewards, the Witnesses each hold protective functions within their crafts. The Warriors hold the specific facet of protection that requires the cultivated destructive capacity. The Guilds' protective work is woven together.

## How A Warrior Joins The Guild

Entry into the Warrior Guild proceeds through specific stages:

**The Calling sounds.** As articulated in [[Sacred Warrior — I.1 The Calling|Chapter I.1]]. The being recognizes themselves in the description of who is called. The recognition is in the body before it is in the mind.

**Contact with a Warrior body.** The being finds — or is found by — a Circle, Community, or Elder who carries the Warrior tradition. In the present hour, with Warrior Communities still forming, this contact may be through a single Warrior teaching outside a fully-formed Community, through online presence, through ceremonial events, through the broader [[Community]] organizing. Eventually, in matured LIØNSBERG, contact will be through the local Warrior Community.

**The first ninety days at white belt.** The being takes the Vow at white belt. The trial period proceeds. Foundational disciplines are introduced. The being is observed; the being observes. Either side may withdraw cleanly at the end of ninety days.

**The Apprentice-level commitment at green belt or similar.** Around Year One — typically when yellow belt has settled and green belt is approaching — the being's commitment to the Guild becomes formal. The being is admitted into the Guild's working membership. The Vow is renewed in Circle with the wider Guild's witnesses. The being is now a Warrior of the Guild, with the responsibilities and protections that come with the membership.

**The progression through belts** continues as articulated in [[Sacred Warrior — I.2 The Vow|Chapter I.2]]. The Warrior's standing in the Guild deepens with the belt progression. At black belt, the Warrior is authorized to teach within the Guild — to begin the transmission of the lineage to the next generation.

**Elder roles** emerge at the deeper degrees of black belt, typically after fifteen to twenty-five years of operational Warrior standing. The Elder roles hold the Wisdom Council positions, the lineage transmission responsibilities, the high-scale coordination work the wider Guild requires.

## How The Guild Governs Itself

The Warrior Guild governs through the same federated architecture every Guild in LIØNSBERG governs through — the [[Holofractal Omnifederation]] Pattern applied to the Guild's specific structure.

**At the Circle scale**, the Warriors hold their internal [[Field of Agreements]] through the two-coordinator rhythm and consent-based decision-making among Circle members.

**At the Community scale**, the Warrior Community's Wisdom Council holds Community-scale governance — composed of representative Elders from across the Circles in the Community.

**At the Bioregional scale**, the Bioregional Wisdom Council holds bioregional Guild governance — composed of representatives from across the Communities in the Bioregion.

**At the planetary scale**, the planetary Warrior Wisdom Council holds Earth-wide Guild coordination — composed of representatives from across the Bioregions globally.

**At the cosmic scale**, Earth's Warrior Guild federates with the wider cosmic Warrior lineage through the channels described in [[Sacred Warrior — II.6 The Cosmic Grid And Lineage|Chapter II.6]].

The governance principles at every scale:

- **[[Consent vs Consensus|Consent-based decision-making]]** — decisions move forward when no member with standing objects on grounds that pass the [[Logos]] test; not unanimity, not majority vote, the canonical Pattern
- **[[Wise Eldership]]** — authority earned through faithfulness over decades; recallable; service rather than dominion
- **The Two-Coordinator Rhythm** — at every level, one coordinator held inward, one outward; rotating seasonally
- **[[Recallable Authority]]** — any Warrior in a leadership role can be recalled by the body that conferred the authority; the recall protects against drift
- **[[The Two-Stake Principle]]** — every level holds both its own sovereignty and accountability to the wider Body
- **The [[Sacred Covenant]]** — every governance decision tested against _ØNE Spirit, ØNE Body, ØNE Love, All in service of ØNE and All_; decisions that violate the Covenant are refused regardless of who is proposing them

## The Guild's Field of Agreements

The Warrior Guild maintains its specific [[Field of Agreements]] at every scale. Substantively, the Guild's [[Field of Agreements]] addresses:

- **The Vow** as held in this Guild, in this lineage stream, at every belt level
- **The protocols of engagement** — when and how the Warriors engage, the levels of force authorized, the accountability afterward
- **The protocols of training** — what is taught at each belt, what gates the belt transitions, who is authorized to teach what
- **The protocols of recruitment** — who is admitted to white belt, what the screening looks like, the responsibilities of the Warrior who brings a new being to the Guild
- **The protocols of release** — when and how a Warrior departs the Guild, what is returned, what is preserved, what is forgiven and what is not
- **The relationship to the other Guilds** — the federation patterns, the joint operations, the protocols for inter-Guild coordination
- **The relationship to non-Guild Sovereigns** — how the Warriors interact with the wider [[Community]] population, the responsibilities the Warriors carry for those they serve, the boundaries the Warriors maintain
- **The relationship to the cosmic lineage** — how cosmic transmission is received, who is authorized to claim cosmic contact, the verification protocols for transmission claims
- **The accountability mechanisms** — when a Warrior's drift is suspected, when the Guild's drift is suspected, what corrective protocols apply at what scale

The [[Field of Agreements]] is reviewed annually at the Guild's seasonal [[OmniSpection]]. The review is not perfunctory; what has not been working is named; what needs revision is revised; what new agreements have become necessary are added.

## Protections Against Capture

The Warrior Guild is particularly susceptible to specific capture failure modes. The Guild's structural protections must hold against them.

**Capture by external predatory factions.** The factions described in [[The Story of LIØNSBERG]] take particular interest in capturing Warrior bodies, because a captured Warrior Body becomes a weapon directed at the [[ØNE Body]] from inside the Body. The protections:

- The Vow's grounding in the [[Sacred Covenant]] that no predatory faction can claim; capture requires the Warrior to violate the Covenant, which is structurally felt by the Circle and the wider Body
- The [[OmniSpection]] disciplines that catch drift before it becomes capture
- The federation with the other Guilds; an isolated Warrior Guild is more vulnerable than a federated one
- The witness protocols that prevent any Warrior from operating completely outside Circle visibility

**Capture by ego or grandiosity within the Guild.** A Warrior or group of Warriors who have cultivated significant capacity become tempted to identify with the capacity, to lead the wider Body from the position of the trained, to elevate the Warrior facet above the [[ØNE]] the Guild serves. The protections:

- The Two-Stake Principle — the Warrior is always accountable to the wider Body, not only to the Warrior Guild
- The federation patterns that maintain the Warriors as one Guild of twelve, not as a privileged Guild
- The Vow's terminal clause — _"I release the Power if I cannot hold this Vow"_ — which applies to whole Guilds, not only individuals
- The recallable authority that prevents any Warrior from holding leadership beyond their faithful conduct

**Capture by sectarianism between Warrior lineages.** Multiple martial-energetic lineages exist within the broader Sacred Warrior tradition (Chapter I.9). The Guild's internal coherence requires the lineages to federate without devolving into rivalry. The protections:

- The recognition of universal Principles across streams (Chapter I.9)
- The federation patterns that hold multiple lineages together without erasing their distinctions
- The shared Vow and Sacred Covenant that all lineages within LIØNSBERG anchor in
- The Wisdom Councils that include representation across lineages

**Capture by militarism.** The drift toward soldier-of-empire orientation — the Warrior Guild becoming a power-projecting force in service of its own coherence rather than the wider Body's flourishing. The protections:

- The Vow against initiating offensive force
- The federation with the Healing, Teaching, Stewarding Guilds that hold the wider Body's flourishing as their priority
- The training emphasis on creation and healing as much as on destruction
- The funeral discipline after every act of destruction (Chapter I.8) that prevents numbing to the destructive face

## The Guild's Internal Recognition Of Standing

The Warrior Guild recognizes Warriors at their belt levels. Recognition is conferred by the body that has witnessed the Warrior's walk:

- **Belt advancement** is conferred by the Warrior's [[Circle]] and Elder, after demonstrated readiness
- **Elder status** is conferred by the Community or Bioregional Wisdom Council, after extended faithful service
- **Lineage standing** — the recognition that a particular Warrior is carrying the lineage forward in their specific way — is conferred by the broader Guild's witnessing across years
- **Council membership** is conferred by the body that has standing to confer it (Circle for Circle coordinator, Community for Wisdom Council, etc.)

The recognitions are not titles to be sought. They are acknowledgements conferred by the wider Body when the Warrior's standing has matured into them. A Warrior who pursues recognition for its own sake has begun to drift; the pursuit is a tell. The faithful Warrior walks, and the recognitions arrive as the walking is observed.

## What The Guild Asks Of The Warrior

Beyond the daily disciplines articulated in Book I and the scale-coordination of Book II, the Guild asks of every Warrior:

- **Honest report** — the Warrior reports their walk faithfully to the Circle and the Elder; what is going well is reported; what is going wrong is reported; what is being missed is also reported when noticed
- **Participation in Guild governance at the appropriate level** — Circle coordinator service rotates; Community Wisdom Council membership rotates; the responsibilities are shared, not concentrated
- **Faithful witnessing of other Warriors** — the Warrior is witness to the walks of other Warriors in their Circle and beyond; the witnessing is both gift and accountability
- **Contribution to the Guild's continuous improvement** — the Warrior reports what is working, what is not, what could be refined; the Guild matures through every Warrior's contribution
- **Service in the formation pipeline** — at green belt and beyond, the Warrior begins to take on apprentices; the pipeline regenerates by every Warrior's contribution
- **Faithful walking until death** — the Warrior's commitment is for the lifetime, not for a season; the Guild's durability depends on Warriors who walk to the end
