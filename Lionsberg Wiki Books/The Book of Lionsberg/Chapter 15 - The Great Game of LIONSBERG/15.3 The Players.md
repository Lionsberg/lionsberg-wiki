# 15.3 The Players

Who plays the Great Game?

**Everyone who chooses to.**

There are no prerequisites. No qualifications. No application. No dues. No test to pass. No doctrine to believe. No leader to follow.

If you can love, you can play.  
If you can help, you can play.  
If you can keep a promise, you can play.  
If you are alive, you can play.

---

Children play — by being kind, by sharing, by asking _"Why?"_ and refusing to accept cruelty as normal.

Families play — by turning their home into a sanctuary, their table into a commons, their love into a force that radiates outward.

Elders play — by carrying wisdom, by tending the young, by remembering what matters when the world forgets.

The sick play — by receiving care with grace and teaching the healthy what truly matters.

The strong play — by protecting those who cannot yet protect themselves.

The broken play — by letting themselves be mended, and letting their mending become medicine for others.

The dying play — by showing the living how to face the great threshold with dignity and peace.

---

Every living being is a potential Player. Every conscious creature has a role. The Game is not reserved for saints, heroes, or the spiritually advanced. It is for everyone — because everyone is needed.

The only beings who are not playing are those who have not yet chosen to.

And the choice is always available. At any moment. In any condition. From any starting point.

**No one is too small. No one is too broken. No one is too late.**

The Game has been waiting for exactly you, exactly as you are, since before the world was formed.
