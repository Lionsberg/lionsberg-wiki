# 15.2 The Goal

The Goal of the Game is the simplest Goal there is:

**ONE. Creator's Intent. Heaven. On Earth. For All. Forever.**

Not Heaven after you die. Heaven here. Heaven now. Heaven progressively realized in every family, every community, every ecosystem, every relationship, every system, every moment — across the entire Earth and beyond.

What does Heaven look like? You already know.

It looks like children who are fed, loved, and free.
It looks like elders who are honored and cared for.
It looks like communities where every person has meaningful work and genuine belonging.
It looks like rivers running clear and forests teeming with life.
It looks like justice without cruelty and mercy without weakness.
It looks like families gathered around a table with enough for all.
It looks like strangers welcomed and enemies reconciled.
It looks like the [[Creator's Intent]] made manifest in every domain of life.

You do not need a theology degree to recognize Heaven. A five-year-old knows what it looks like. She points to it every time she says _"That's not fair"_ or _"Why is that person hungry?"_ or _"Can't we help?"_

---

The Goal is not a destination you arrive at and stop. It is an infinite direction — always closer, never finished. Like tending a garden. Like raising a child. Like loving someone more deeply with each passing year.

Every move in the Game is measured by one question:

_Does this make life a little more like Heaven, or a little more like Hell?_

If the answer is Heaven — make the move.
If the answer is Hell — stop, turn, and choose again.

That is the entire compass of the Game. It works for a child choosing how to treat a friend. It works for a community choosing how to govern itself. It works for a civilization choosing its future.

**One Goal. One Direction. One Question. At every scale. In every world.**
