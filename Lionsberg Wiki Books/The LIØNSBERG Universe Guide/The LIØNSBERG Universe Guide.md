## Part II — The Voice

_How LIØNSBERG speaks. The prophetic-operational register._

### Sub-sections (to be authored)

- **The LIØNSBERG Voice** — the wise elder at the fire coupled with the general of cosmic armies; prophetic register with operational precision; scripture that contains program management; sensory and abstract in tension; staccato for weight; liturgical parallelism
- **Voice Discipline — What To Refuse** — AI-tells; tutorial register; "this is not X, this is Y" defensive constructions; vague openers ("Something miraculous happens when..."); raising-doubt-to-knock-down constructions; encyclopedia-form templates
- **No Source-Elevation** — no tradition, source, scripture, or external author is elevated above others as the lens through which LIØNSBERG must be read. The canonical voice draws on all traditions through cross-tradition resonance; none is privileged as authority
- **No Religion-Elevation** — no specific religious tradition is the framing lens for canonical articulations. Historical-reflection chapters may name traditions and figures as historical actors; canonical articulations universalize
- **No Person-Elevation in Conventions** — canonical disciplines and practices are named LIØNSBERG-native (e.g., [[The Card Method]] not "The Pirsig Method"); the practice belongs, the source-anchored name doesn't

- **Tone Calibration** — definitive not tentative ("it is" not "it is likely"); active present-tense verbs; bold key concepts; balance sacred and practical, urgent and hopeful, cosmic and actionable

[[The LIØNSBERG Voice]] is the canonical full standard. This Part of the Guide is the **summary** and the **why** behind it.

## Part VI — Production Conventions

_The practical housekeeping disciplines that keep the wiki coherent at the file level._

This Part is more practical than the Parts above — the actual file-level conventions that make the wiki coherent in Obsidian. Authors should know Parts I–V deeply; the conventions in this Part are reference material consulted when doing wiki work.

### Sub-sections (to be authored)

- **File Structure Policy** — the main page for each wiki book lives **inside** the wiki book folder (acts as opening / TOC). Applied consistently across all wiki books we keep.
- **No Duplicate Filenames** — Obsidian resolves wiki links by filename only. Never create two files with the same name anywhere in the wiki. Use scope-bearing unique names (e.g., `Playbook Fragments For Synthesis.md` not `README.md`)
- **Wiki Link Convention** — always use `[[wiki links]]` for internal file references; never use markdown links or file paths with `.md` extensions. Obsidian resolves by filename regardless of location
- **Two-Trailing-Spaces Discipline** — two trailing spaces at the end of a line create a `<br>` line break in rendered markdown. Required for: bare wiki links on their own lines, navigation footers, poetic/liturgical lines, any non-bulleted line meant to stand alone. NOT required for: bulleted lines, regular prose paragraphs, headers, blank lines, horizontal rules
- **NBSP Cleanup** — the Obsidian editor and copy-paste operations introduce Non-Breaking Space characters (U+00A0). These look invisible but break search/replace and markdown parsing. Periodic cleanup: `grep -rlP '\xc2\xa0' --include="*.md"` to find them
- **Capitalization Conventions** — LIØNSBERG always all-caps with Ø glyph; ØNE wherever it appears (not "The ØNE" — sentence grammar handles the article); Self capitalized when referring to the deeper Self; key concepts bolded in prose
- **The Ø Glyph** — adopted as canonical Phase 1.5 of the Great Spring 2026 Wiki Turn. Used in: LIØNSBERG, ØNE, and selectively in other canonical proper names where the glyph carries meaning
- **The 𓂀 Hieroglyph** — used as section marker in scripture-grade prophetic-liturgical text (e.g., THE GREAT RETURN); not in standard prose
- **Frontmatter Conventions** — `epistemic_status:` (to be adopted, decision D9 pending) with 6 values: empirical / logical-inference / transmitted-witness-grade / hypothesis / archetypal-true-myth / convention. Card frontmatter includes status, themes, source, captured-date
- **[[The Card Method]]** — capture and emergence; one idea per Card; resist premature structure; list-Card sub-rule for curated enumerations
- **Lifecycle Tags** — pages may carry maturity stage: seed / sprout / seedling / sapling / adult
- **Personal-Reserved Discipline** — biographical-specific material, family/named-individual content, specific dollar figures, dated political content, post-death-event biographical declarations stay personal; universal patterns are lifted into canonical content
- **Git Discipline** — pull before working sessions; commit frequently; push to backup; preserve provenance; archive rather than delete when in doubt
