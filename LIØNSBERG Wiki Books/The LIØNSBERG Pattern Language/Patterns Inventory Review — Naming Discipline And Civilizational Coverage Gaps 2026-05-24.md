# Part 1 — Naming Discipline

## The Failure Mode

I applied **verbal-gerund mechanically** to states that are not activities. The form "X-ing" only carries wisdom alive when X is something the Sovereign **does** — not when X is something the Sovereign **is** or **undergoes**.

Examples of the failure:
- "Aweing" — Awe is not an activity. The Sovereign **stands in** awe; awe **arises**. The wise right name is **Awe**.
- "Joying" — Joy is a state that flows. "Joying" reads like a Bureau-of-Standards rename. The wise right name is **Joy**.
- "Disgusting" — In English, "disgusting" modifies the object, not the experiencer. The wise right name is **Disgust** (or **Reading Disgust** for the discipline of attending to it).
- "Empathying" — English fails this verb. The wise right name is **Empathy** (or **Empathic Resonance**).

The mechanical verbing reads as forced linguistic invention, not as a living name. It violates the [[The LIØNSBERG Voice]] discipline of trusting the reader and letting names do their natural work.

## The Wise Right Three Forms

Patterns can take one of three name-forms based on what kind of wisdom-unit they hold:

### Form A — Nominal (For Aspects, States, Named Qualities, Realities)

Use when the Pattern names **what something is** — a state the Sovereign enters; a quality of consciousness; a structural reality.

Examples:
- **Awe** · **Joy** · **Grief** · **Sadness** · **Fear** · **Anger** · **Love** · **Peace** · **Reverence**
- **The Sovereign's Body** · **The Energetic Anatomy** · **The Field**
- **Stillness As The Floor** · **The Caves** · **The Way**

### Form B — Verbal-Gerund (For Cultivation Activities, Active Practices, Disciplines)

Use when the Pattern names **what the Sovereign does** — a sustained, embodied cultivation activity; an active discipline that lives in the doing.

Examples:
- **Eating** · **Walking** · **Sleeping** · **Breathing** · **Fasting** · **Moving** · **Tending**
- **Loving** · **Listening** · **Witnessing** · **Forgiving** · **Federating** · **Pass-Flaming**
- **Cultivating** · **Discerning** · **Stewarding**

### Form C — Composed Phrase (For Disciplines Around A State, Where Bare Nominal Or Bare Gerund Both Fail)

Use when the Pattern is **a discipline of meeting / cultivating / transmuting a state** — and the bare state-name is too passive while the bare gerund is forced.

Examples:
- **Meeting Despair** (not "Despairing", not just "Despair")
- **Cultivating Gratitude** (not "Gratituding")
- **Transmuting Shame** (not "Shaming")
- **Reading Disgust** (not "Disgusting")
- **Channeling Fury** (not "Furying")
- **Sitting With Anguish** (not "Anguishing")
- **Integrating Fear** (not "Fearing")
- **Releasing Contempt** (not "Contempting")

The composed phrase carries both the state (the nominal anchor) and the discipline (the gerund) without forcing English into shapes it cannot hold.

## The Diagnostic

When considering a title:

1. **Is this what the Sovereign IS or UNDERGOES?** → Form A (nominal).
2. **Is this what the Sovereign DOES sustainedly?** → Form B (verbal-gerund).
3. **Is this a discipline of meeting / cultivating / transmuting something the Sovereign IS or UNDERGOES?** → Form C (composed phrase).

The test: read the candidate aloud. If it feels alive, use it. If it feels like a linguistic invention forced past a natural shape, redo.

The deeper rule: **the language must serve the wisdom, never the convention.** Verbing was a useful corrective against AI's tendency to noun-everything, but applied mechanically it becomes its own distortion.

### Pillar-Specific Verbings

The Pillar II-XII verb-coinages (Justice-Economizing, Story-Transmitting, etc.) mostly belong in the [[Lexicon]] — they are **lexical verb-forms** of Pillar-functions, not Pattern Cards. The corresponding Patterns are nominal or composed:

- "Justice-Economizing" → **Just Economy** (Pattern) + **justicing** (Lexicon verb)
- "Story-Transmitting" → **Story Transmission** (Pattern) + **storytelling** (Lexicon verb)
- "Sacred-Geometrying" → **Sacred Geometry In Form** (Pattern) + **geometrizing** (Lexicon verb)
- "Restorative-Justicing" → **Restorative Justice** (Pattern) + **justicing** (Lexicon verb)
- "Truth-Reconciling" → **Truth And Reconciliation** (Pattern) + **reconciling** (Lexicon verb)

The Lexicon already holds the verb-form of every Pillar function. The Pattern Language should hold the Patterns by their wise-right names — usually nominal or composed.

### Verb-Coinages Surfaced — Reviewed

The 12 coinages at the end of the backlog are mostly fine; a few need adjustment:

- **Wayfaring** ✓ — alive, retain
- **Thresholding** ✓ — retain as the active form of [[Crossing A Threshold]]
- **Patterning** ✓ — retain (the active composition of Patterns in the moment)
- **Belt-ing** — awkward; better as a description (**Being Recognized At The Blue Belt**) or the broader Pattern name [[Belt Progression As Recognized Embodiment]]. Drop the standalone verb.
- **Sovereigning** — works, but is rare in canonical use; consider **Acting As Sovereign** as paired
- **Oning / ØNE-ing** — awkward. Better: **Orienting To ØNE** or **The Direct Relationship**
- **Logos-Recognizing** — awkward. Better: **Recognizing The Logos**
- **Witnessing** ✓ — retain (already an English word that carries the wisdom)
- **Federating** ✓ — retain (in active use)
- **Hubbing / Arking** — awkward. Better: **Building A Hub** and **Building An Ark**
- **Questing** ✓ — retain (the active form of [[Choose A Quest]])
- **Pass-Flaming** — awkward. Better: **Passing The Flame** (the canonical phrase)

**General rule for coined verbs**: only coin where English fails AND the resulting verb is naturally pronounceable AND it doesn't fight the established noun. "Wayfaring" works because "wayfarer" is already English. "Belt-ing" fails because the noun "belt" is too dominant.
