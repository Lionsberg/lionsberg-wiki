# PDG - The IPD Agreement and Core Group

## 3.1 Site Project Management

The Program Manager, with assistance from the SLE, will establish the space program for each site. The IPD team(s) selected to deliver that project will use elements from the Prototype "Kit of Parts" and adapt them to that particular site. Delivery will be managed by a Core Group comprised of leads from the Program Manager and IPD Teams.

## 3.1.1 The IPD Agreement

The project delivery method involves a contractual combination of "Lean Project Delivery" and an integrated team. A very similar method was developed by Northern California's Sutter Health for its $6.5 billion building program.

**Development of the IPD Agreement for Lean Project Delivery**: The agreement describes the parties' relationship throughout the life of the Program using underlying principles of Lean Project Delivery. It is plainly written for all participants.

**Relationship of the Parties**: The IPD Agreement defines roles and responsibilities reaching deep into the project delivery team. Rather than the traditional "three legged stool," the relationship is depicted as three overlapping circles recognizing each party needs to collaborate with others.

**The Core Group**: Functions like a corporate executive team. Decisions made by consensus. Senior executives participate at least quarterly. Only in event of impasse does resolution transfer to the Owner.

**Creating a Collaborative Environment**: Collaboration occurs best when participants view themselves as equal. The IPD Agreement states: "In order to achieve the Owner's basic value proposition, design of the Project must proceed with informed, accurate information concerning program, quality, cost and schedule."

**Target Value Design**: The Core Group develops a TVD plan making value, cost, schedule and constructability basic design criteria.

**Network of Commitments**: Ballard and Howell found that only about half of assignments made to construction crews at the beginning of a week were completed when planned. By utilizing the Last Planner System, they found parties could drive the successful commitment rate to between 80% and 90% on a weekly basis.

The IPD Agreement provides that if the IPD Team has maximized pre-construction collaboration, there will be little or no need for RFIs after construction. When clarification is needed, the party should first raise the issue in a face-to-face conversation or via telephone.

**Planning System**: Based on collaborative pull planning using the Last Planner System. At a minimum: milestone schedule, collaboratively created phase schedules, "make-ready" look-ahead plans, weekly work plans, and a method for measuring planning reliability.

**Optimizing the Project, not the Pieces**: The Agreement seeks to create shared risk, reducing overall project risk rather than shifting it. Early involvement of the project team eliminates ambiguity. General contractor compensated on cost-plus fee basis. Change orders limited to material scope changes, changed site conditions, or unforeseen regulatory interpretations.

**Risk Sharing**: The Owner and Core Group members agree that for costs in excess of the IPD Team's Performance Contingency, the IPD Team's profit will be at risk for design errors or omissions, whether or not negligent. This allows parties to share risk without being forced into an adversarial system.

**Tightly Couple Learning With Action**: Planning system calls for weekly assessments of planning reliability and reasons for variance. Monthly assessments of root causes of contingency utilization. The Core Group is charged with developing project evaluation criteria, conducting periodic assessments, and planning programs to improve performance.

## 3.1.2 Core Group

Each site Project is managed by a Core Group. The traditional "command and control" approach is inconsistent with IPD and cannot achieve the efficiency benefits of an integrated process. The success depends on the performance of the entire team.

**Mission**: Deliver maximum value by utilizing IPD approaches implementing Lean. Create collaborative partnerships where team members are jointly responsible using the Last Planner System. Encourage reliable promises and continuous improvement.

**Membership**: At a minimum, representatives from the Design-Builder and the Program Manager. Other members may include key trade partners. Membership changes over time. Optimum size is 4-6 members. Not led by any one individual -- all members are equal in stature.

**Activities**: Regularly invite performers and leadership for progress reviews. Manage production through the Last Planner System. Invite future participants to meetings at least three months prior to active participation.

**Leadership Model**: Trust is the foundation. Leadership shared. Decisions consensus-based. Meetings facilitated by PM's Project Lead or neutral facilitator. Agenda topics: safety, financial management, schedule, personnel, quality review, lean practices, lessons learned.

**Responsibilities**: Create and sustain a community of Lean Practice. Work in alignment with each other despite company loyalties. Create a culture in which:

- All participants understand and commit to collaboration
- Focus is on team performance with open, straight and honest communication
- Responsibilities clearly defined in a no-blame culture
- Each participant valued in a learning environment
- All required to make "reliable promises" and use the Last Planner System
- New leaders developed by valuing good performance
- Incentives administered fairly
- Risk collectively managed

Also responsible for:

- Adapting the PDG to address project-specific issues
- Putting the appropriate functional organization structure in place
- Overseeing the Risk/Incentive plan
- Performance of the overall project and all project members
- Determining reporting responsibilities

**Behaviors**: Members must exhibit behaviors they expect from the rest of the organization:

1. **Communication** -- "The Free Exchange of Ideas" -- fostering free exchange irrespective of role
2. **Collaboration** -- "Creating the Collaborative Environment" -- Trust creates atmosphere for free exchange. Dialogue respectful, parties distinguish facts from assessments.
3. **Leadership** -- "Establishing a Community of Practice" -- Members visible and emulate desired behavior. Encourage new ideas. Facilitate continuing learning in lean and IPD.

The Core Group must exhibit these behaviors not only in meetings but throughout the site.
