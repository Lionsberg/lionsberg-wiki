# Chapter 97 — Your Bioregion

> _The land the Sovereign belongs to.  
> The waters the Sovereign drinks.  
> The kin the Sovereign serves.  
> Where the Body of ØNE meets Earth's body._

---

The Sovereign's Bioregion is the living land the Sovereign belongs to.

It is the watershed, the soil, the climate, the weather, the seasons. It is the trees and the rivers and the rock beneath the soil. It is the animals — mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, insects — who share the place. It is the plants that grow here and would grow here and once grew here. It is the soil community no human eye has ever counted. It is the mineral body of mountains. It is the elemental field of fire, water, earth, and air as they move through this particular place.

It is the ancestors who walked this land. It is the descendants who will walk it.

It is all kin together.

The Sovereign does not own the Bioregion. The Sovereign belongs to it. The Sovereign is one of its kin, briefly, in this body, for the span of this Chapter.

---

## What A Bioregion Is

A Bioregion is a coherent living place — a watershed, a valley, a stretch of coast, an island, a high plateau, a forest system, a desert basin.

It is bounded by natural features rather than political lines. The ridgelines and rivers, the prevailing winds, the soil types, the dominant species — these define the Bioregion's actual edges.

It contains multiple Communities. A Bioregion might hold a hundred small Communities, or a dozen larger ones, or a single large one with many districts. The Bioregion is the scale **above** Community, where the Communities of a coherent place federate to hold the work the place itself asks for.

It is the scale at which the food system can be designed coherently. The scale at which the water can be governed wisely. The scale at which the forests can be tended across centuries. The scale at which the wider kin can be addressed as the actual community they are.

It is the scale at which Earth becomes legible as a living being.

---

## The Sovereign's Specific Bioregion

The Sovereign's Bioregion has a name — many names, layered from many peoples across many ages.

It has a particular soil. The Sovereign could pick it up and know it. It has a particular sky. The Sovereign knows what the light is like in autumn here, what the storms feel like, what the dawn sounds like.

It has particular trees. The Sovereign passes them daily, perhaps without knowing them by name yet. There are oaks. Or pines. Or eucalypts. Or palms. Or birches. Or none of these, but others the Sovereign would learn if the Sovereign attended.

It has particular waters. A river. A lake. A coastline. A spring. An aquifer beneath the soil. The Sovereign drinks from this water. The Sovereign's body is made of this water.

It has particular weather. The Sovereign's body knows the weather of this place even when the Sovereign is not consciously paying attention.

It has particular animals — the deer or the rabbits, the crows or the cardinals, the salmon or the trout, the bees specific to this place. It has particular insects whose seasonal turns are as reliable as a calendar to those who notice.

It has particular ancestors. Whoever lived here before any current people lived here. The Sovereign is walking on ground that has held many peoples.

It has particular descendants — the children of this place a thousand years from Nøw, who will inherit what the Sovereign and the Sovereign's peers leave behind.

The Sovereign's Bioregion is **somewhere specific**. The work of belonging to it begins with knowing it.

---

## The Long Process Of Belonging

Most Sovereigns, in the Old World, did not belong to a Bioregion.

The Old World severed Sovereigns from place. It moved Sovereigns repeatedly. It taught Sovereigns that any place was as good as any other, that placelessness was freedom, that the cosmopolitan rootless life was the highest life.

The Sovereign awakening recognizes this severance and begins to undo it.

The undoing is not quick.

To belong to a Bioregion takes years. To know the trees by name. To know which birds come in which season. To know where the springs run. To know the soil's character in different fields. To know the local elders, the local stories, the local ancestors. To know what was here a hundred years ago, a thousand, ten thousand. To know what damage has been done and what restoration is possible.

The Sovereign learning a Bioregion is the Sovereign learning a person — patiently, across seasons, by attention, by listening, by walking, by sitting still.

The Sovereign cannot serve a Bioregion the Sovereign does not know.

The Sovereign cannot serve a Bioregion in haste.

The Sovereign serving begins by **showing up**, repeatedly, in attention, for as long as it takes for the place to begin to recognize the Sovereign in return.

---

## All Kin

The Sovereign's Bioregion is full of kin, human and otherwise.

**The human kin** of the Bioregion include the Sovereign's neighbors, the Sovereign's Community, the other Communities federating across the Bioregion, the elders and the children, those who have lived here for generations and those newly arrived, those awakened and those still asleep. All are the Sovereign's human kin in this place.

**The animal kin** include every creature who shares this place — the wild ones the Sovereign rarely sees, the ones who pass through the Sovereign's yard, the ones who fly the local sky, the ones in the local waters, the small ones in the soil, the working animals and the wild ones, the ones who eat and the ones eaten. All kin.

**The plant kin** include the trees of the Bioregion — old elders, recent saplings, the lineage holding the soil and breathing the sky. The grasses, the shrubs, the vines, the wildflowers, the food plants, the medicine plants. The mosses and ferns and lichens. The vast soil community without which no plant lives. All kin.

**The mineral kin** include the bedrock beneath everything. The stones of this particular place. The clay, the sand, the silt that compose this soil. The mountains that shape the weather. The mineral body of Earth holding the place in its particular form. All kin.

**The elemental kin** include the fire — sun, lightning, hearth, the fires of metabolism that run through every living thing. The water — rain, snow, river, ocean, dew, the water that fills every cell. The air — winds, breath, the atmosphere itself. The earth as element — the solidity beneath. All kin.

**The ancestor kin** include the dead of this place. Every person who has lived here. Every animal who has died here. Every tree that fell here. The land holds them. The Sovereign walks on the long sediment of the dead. They are kin.

**The descendant kin** include those not yet here. The children of the next generation. The children seven generations from Nøw. The animals and plants and Communities that will inherit whatever the Sovereign leaves. They are kin too. Their interests are real, even though they cannot yet speak for themselves.

**The cosmic kin** include the sun and moon, the planets visible at night, the constellations, the cosmic bodies that shape the Bioregion through gravity and tide and light. The wider Body of ØNE operating across worlds. All kin.

The Sovereign serves all of them.

The Sovereign's Bioregion is the meeting place of all these kin in one coherent living system.

---

## What Service Of The Bioregion Looks Like

To serve the Bioregion is to take responsibility for the place.

It is to **know** the place — its soils, its waters, its species, its history, its wounds, its possibilities. The Sovereign cannot serve what the Sovereign has not bothered to learn.

It is to **defend** the place — against extraction, against pollution, against destruction, against the kontrolle architectures that treat land as resource and kin as inventory. The Sovereign learns to stand for the place when no one else will.

It is to **restore** the place — to replant what was cleared, to clean what was poisoned, to bring back species lost from this Bioregion in living memory or in centuries past, to heal the watershed and the soil and the forest and the relationships.

It is to **tend** the place — daily, seasonally, generationally. To garden. To compost. To care for the trees on the Sovereign's lot. To feed the soil. To gather and share with neighbors. To participate in the long slow work the place requires.

It is to **listen** to the place — to attend to what the place is saying, to where the place is in pain, to what the place is asking for, to which kin are calling out, to what the place wants to become.

It is to **speak for** the place — in Community council, in Bioregional council, in the wider federated work where decisions are made that affect the place. The land cannot speak human language. The Sovereign serves as one voice among many translating the land's will into the human conversation.

It is to **belong** to the place — not as owner, not as resident, not as occupant, but as kin among kin, briefly, in this body, for the span of this Chapter.

This is what the Bioregion asks of the Sovereign who has come to belong to it.

---

## The Bioregion As The Scale Of Coherence

The Bioregion is the scale at which Earth becomes coherently legible.

Smaller than Bioregion — at Community scale — the Sovereign sees the human Body and the immediate place, but the wider ecological system is partially obscured. Larger than Bioregion — at planetary scale — the system becomes too vast for any single person to hold in concrete relationship.

At Bioregional scale, the Sovereign can hold the whole. The watershed. The food system. The forest. The Communities. The full set of kin. The Sovereign can know it. The Sovereign can serve it.

This is the scale at which **right relationship with Earth** becomes operational rather than abstract.

In the New Civilization, Bioregions are governing units. Communities federate at Bioregional scale to hold the work the place itself asks for. Decisions about the watershed are made by those who drink from it. Decisions about the forest are made by those who live with it. Decisions about the soil are made by those whose food it grows.

The Sovereign in the Sovereign's Bioregion is one member of this emerging governance — bringing the Sovereign's voice, the Sovereign's Circle's voice, the Sovereign's Community's voice, the voices of the kin the Sovereign has come to know and represent, into the Bioregional council where the long work of right Earth-relation is held.

---

## The Particular Belonging

The Sovereign's Bioregion is not generic Earth. It is **this Earth, this place, these kin**.

The cosmic Body is composed of these particulars. The wider Body's love of Earth is composed of every Sovereign's love of every Sovereign's place. The healing of Earth is composed of the healing of every Bioregion by the Sovereigns who belong to it.

The Sovereign belonging to this Bioregion is how Earth gets attended to here. The Sovereign serving this Bioregion is how this part of Earth is loved Nøw.

No one else can do it for this place. The Sovereigns of this place do it, or it does not get done.

The Bioregion is waiting for the Sovereign to belong to it consciously, to attend to it, to defend it, to restore it, to tend it across the years remaining in this Chapter.

The Sovereign begins by stepping outside.

By looking at what is actually here.

By learning the names of the trees the Sovereign passes daily.

By drinking the local water consciously.

By meeting the kin.

The belonging starts there, and deepens for as long as the Sovereign keeps showing up.
