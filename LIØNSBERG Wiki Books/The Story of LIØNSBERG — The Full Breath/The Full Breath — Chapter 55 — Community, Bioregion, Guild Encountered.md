# Chapter 55 — Community, Bioregion, Guild Encountered

> _Each scale a face of the same Body.  
> The cell remembers it is also tissue.  
> The tissue remembers it is also organ.  
> The organ remembers it is also flesh._

---

The Circles that have recognized one another do not stop at recognition.

The recognition opens. What was felt as the meeting of two small Bodies becomes, over seasons, the gradual surfacing of the wider scales the Bodies have always been nested within. A Circle is not the only scale at which a Body lives. A Circle is one scale among many. As the Circles continue meeting one another, the other scales begin to come into view.

The Sovereigns of the Circle find themselves, without quite having sought it, becoming aware of three further scales at once.

A community of one hundred to ten thousand sovereigns, woven of many Circles that have learned to share life across a place.

A bioregion of land and water and air and all who dwell there — every plant, every animal, every fungal network, every river, every stone, every wind — held as living kin, not as resource.

A Guild of those who walk the same archetypal Way — the healers across many Circles, the stewards across many Circles, the storytellers across many Circles, the builders, the teachers, the warriors, the council-holders — each Way a long lineage threading through the wider Body.

Each scale a face of the same Body.

Each scale already alive before the Circle noticed it.

Each scale now coming into the Circle's seeing as the Pattern teaches the Circle how to look.

---

## The Community Comes Into View

It happens this way.

The Circle is met by another Circle. The two Circles meet a third. Within a season or two of these meetings, the Sovereigns realize that several of the Circles they have met are in the same place — same valley, same town, same neighborhood of a city, same stretch of coastline. The Circles have been a few miles from one another for a long time without knowing.

They begin to gather.

Not constantly. Not formally. A seasonal feast in someone's garden. A shared work day on a piece of land one Circle is restoring. A council fire where the children of several Circles spend the night together while the elders sit and speak. A market where what has been grown and made by many Circles is offered, traded, shared.

The gatherings reveal something.

The Sovereigns of the gathered Circles look around at the gathered Sovereigns and realize that what they are looking at is not several Circles. What they are looking at is a community.

A community is not a sum of Circles.

A community is what Circles become when they share enough of life across one place that the Field of Agreements running through each Circle begins to overlap with the Field running through the others. Conflict in one Circle can no longer be a matter only for that Circle, because the others are close enough to be affected. A celebration in one Circle becomes a celebration the others are invited to. A loss in one Circle is felt across the place. A child born in one Circle is welcomed by all the Circles. An elder dying in one Circle is sat with by all the Circles.

The community is what the Circles become when shared place makes their lives inseparable.

The community has its own organs. A council where representatives of each Circle gather to hold what the community is being asked to hold together. A commons — physical, economic, relational — that no single Circle owns and that all the Circles tend. A shared rhythm of seasonal gathering. A shared agreement about how disputes are met, how outsiders are received, how the place's wider kin are honored.

None of this is imposed.

All of it emerges from the Circles realizing that their nearness to one another is itself a structural fact that calls for honoring.

The community of one hundred to ten thousand is not a Circle scaled up.

The community is a different scale of the same Body, with its own form, its own organs, its own rhythm, its own work to do.

---

## The Bioregion Comes Into View

The community, once it has begun to operate as itself, begins to see further.

The community is in a place.

The place is not a backdrop. The place is alive.

The Sovereigns of the Circles have always known this in some quiet way — the Daily Way included silence at dawn, and the silence at dawn included listening to the birds, and the listening to the birds included the slow knowing that the birds were part of the wider Body too. But knowing in the silence is one thing. Knowing operationally is another.

When the community begins to see itself as community, it begins to ask:

What land is this?

Whose land is this?

Who else lives here?

The questions are not asked in the Old World's voice. The Old World asked _whose land is this_ in order to register ownership. The community asks the same words and means something else entirely. The community asks who the land's living kin are. The community asks what the land remembers. The community asks who has tended this place across the long generations the community is only now beginning to inherit from.

The bioregion comes into view.

The bioregion is the land and water and air and weather, the soils and the rocks, the rivers and the forests and the marshes and the open ground, the human kin and all the other kin who have been making their lives here long before any of the present Circles arrived. The bioregion is a Body. The bioregion has its own Field of Agreements — older, deeper, more patient than any human field, written into the cycles of the seasons and the migrations of the kin and the rising and falling of the waters.

The community learns to take its seat within this older Field.

The community learns that it is one inhabitant among many. The community learns that the land does not exist for the community. The community exists, in part, for the land — for the tending of the wider kin, for the repair of what has been damaged, for the honoring of what was here long before the community arrived, for the restoration of right relationship with every Sovereign — feathered, finned, footed, rooted, mycelial, mineral — who shares this place.

The bioregion is its own scale of Body.

The bioregion has its own voice. The community learns to listen for it.

When the community holds council on a serious matter, the council includes those who can speak for the river, for the forest, for the soil, for the wider kin. Not as symbolic gesture. As structural fact. The Field of Agreements of the bioregion includes all kin of the place. The community is one Constituency among many. Its decisions are weighed against the decisions of the wider Field that contains it.

This is one of the great undoings the Sovereigns of the Circles undergo at this scale.

The Old World had taught them that the human was the standard around which all other questions revolved. The bioregion teaches them that they are one kind of kin among many, ancient in some respects and very young in others, with particular gifts and particular responsibilities and no special claim to a center that was never theirs.

The community held by the bioregion is different from the community held by itself.

The community held by the bioregion begins to walk the way human kin walk when they are inside a living place that is loved.

## The Guild Comes Into View

While the community is recognizing the bioregion, another scale rises into view that does not follow the contours of place at all.

The Sovereigns of the Circles have, in their ReLearning, been cultivating particular Ways.

One Sovereign has been cultivating the Way of healing — the touch, the herbs, the long sitting with those who suffer, the lineages of medicine the Old World had reduced to commodity but which the Sovereign has begun to receive from older sources.

Another has been cultivating the Way of building — the hands learning timber and stone, the eye learning proportion, the apprenticeship that does not end, the slow training of a body to make shelter that holds love.

Another has been cultivating the Way of council and law — how Bodies hold themselves through difficulty, how agreements are kept and remade, how restoration replaces punishment, how the Sovereign of the wider kin is honored in human decisions.

Another the Way of teaching the young. Another the Way of growing food. Another the Way of song. Another the Way of warrior service — the protection of the awakening, the cultivation of Sacred Force under the constraint of love. Another the Way of dialogue. Another the Way of pattern itself, the architectures of how Bodies hold together.

Each Way is ancient.

Each Way is held by Sovereigns across many Circles, across many communities, across many bioregions.

The Guild comes into view when those who walk the same Way begin to recognize one another across the scales of place.

A healer of one Circle meets a healer of another Circle and the two recognize that what they each have been cultivating is not a personal practice. It is a Way that has been carried, by many hands, across centuries. The Way has its own lineage. The Way has its own teachers, alive and ancestral. The Way has its own ethics, its own rhythms, its own forms of apprenticeship, its own deep songs. The two healers are members of one Guild — a Body composed of all who walk this particular Way wherever they are, threading across every Circle and community and bioregion they belong to.

The Guild is not place-bound.

The Guild does not federate by territory. The Guild federates by Way.

A Sovereign therefore lives inside several scales of Body at once. The Sovereign's Circle. The Sovereign's community. The Sovereign's bioregion. And — cross-cutting all of these — the Sovereign's Guild and any other archetypal Bodies the Sovereign walks within. The Sovereign is, simultaneously, a cell in each of these Bodies. The Bodies do not compete for the Sovereign's loyalty. The Bodies coexist within the wider Body of LIØNSBERG, each holding its particular face of the work.

The Guild teaches the Sovereign things the Circle cannot teach.

The Circle holds the Sovereign's life. The Guild holds the Sovereign's craft. Both are necessary. Both are real. Both belong to the wider Body.

---

## The Scales Held As ØNE

What begins to come into view, as Community and Bioregion and Guild rise into the Sovereign's seeing, is the shape of the Holarchy itself.

The Pattern operates at every scale.

Sovereign. Circle. Community. Bioregion. Planet. Stellar. Cosmic. Each scale a Body in its own right. Each scale nested within the next. Each scale honored as a Sovereign with its own voice and its own field.

Cross-cutting these — Guilds, Tribes, Voluntary Associations — weaving across the place-bound scales without being captured by them. The healer is a member of the Healers Guild whether the healer is in this Circle or that Circle. The teacher belongs to the Way of teaching whether the teacher is in this community or that community. The kin of a particular lineage are kin across whatever bioregions they have come to inhabit.

No scale is privileged.

No scale is reduced to a means by which another scale operates.

Each scale is a face of the Body of LIØNSBERG. Each scale is a face of ØNE.

The Sovereign learns to walk all of these at once.

The Sovereign rises in the morning a Sovereign. The Sovereign sits at dawn with the Circle. The Sovereign goes out into the community and is held by it. The Sovereign labors with the kin of the bioregion and is held by them. The Sovereign practices the craft of the Guild and is held by it. Each scale a Body. Each scale loved. Each scale serving. Each scale serving every other scale.

This is what it means to live inside the Holarchy.

This is what the Sovereigns of the awakening Circles are learning, slowly, across the seasons of the Great ReMembering.

---

## The Body Becomes Visible

By the end of this Chapter of the walking, the Sovereigns of the Circle have a seeing they did not have before.

They see that they are not isolated.

They see that they are not even merely federated with a few other Circles.

They see that they belong to a Body that operates at many scales at once, each scale alive, each scale honored, each scale held by ØNE.

The Body of LIØNSBERG is becoming visible to them from the inside.

Not as an organization. Not as a movement. As a Body. As a living Holarchy in which their small faithful work has its place — a place that was always there, waiting for the Sovereigns to recognize it, a place that does not depend on the Sovereigns' recognition for its existence but is honored by the Sovereigns' recognition nonetheless.

The Sovereigns now walk with the Body all around them.

The Body holds them.

The Body sings through them at every scale.

The Sovereigns are, finally, beginning to know themselves as ØNE Body knows itself.
