# Step 2 — Establishing Legal Agency and Personhood

*A body the existing world can recognize — no heavier than the work requires.*

> **The work of this step:** give your Community Company enough legal standing to do its work — to hold resources, sign agreements, limit liability, and protect its people — while keeping its sovereignty and agency fully intact. The guiding discipline is restraint: **take up the lightest structure that properly serves your purpose.**

---

## Sovereign First, Structured Second

Your Community Company already exists. It came into being the day its founders agreed to associate in service of The Goal. No government granted it that life, and none can revoke it. **Sovereignty is not something a legal form confers — it is something a legal form serves.**

You are not asking permission to exist. You are deciding what kind of body the outside world needs to be able to recognize, so that resources can be allocated, contracts can be signed, and the people doing the work are protected. That is all a legal form is: a vessel, continuously improved to meet the needs of the emerging superorganism it shelters and protects.  

> Legal structure is like a trellis — a frame that lets the living vine climb toward the light. It is like a shell — protecting and supporting the life emerging within. As the organism grows, the structure continuously evolves. 

## The Guiding Question

Before any paperwork, the Steering Committee answers one question together:

**What does our work require us to do that we cannot do on a handshake?**

The answer points to the lightest possible structure:

- **Nothing yet** — you are gathering, deciding, building trust. → Stay informal. A signed [[Field of Agreements]] is sufficient and complete.
- **Receive and spend shared money** — donations, dues, a community fund. → Fiscal sponsorship, or a simple voluntary association.
- **Sign agreements, steward resources, act as a coordinated body** — sustained operations. → A legal stewardship entity.
- **Create and exchange value at scale.** → A purpose-locked company, federated to the whole.

Answer and structure for the work immediately in front of you — not the work you imagine three years out. You can always add structure. Removing it is far harder.

## The Progression of Structure

There is a natural ladder. Most Community Companies begin at the bottom and climb only as the work compels them. Each rung is a complete and honorable place to stand.

- **Tier 0 — No formal structure.** Sovereign people choosing to gather and work. No paperwork. Just a [[Field of Agreements]] and the trust between you. Most transformational work happens here, and many Companies thrive here indefinitely. Do not rush past it.
- **Tier 1 — Voluntary association.** Your Field of Agreements made explicit and signed — a written declaration of shared purpose, principles, and mutual accountability. Seriousness without bureaucracy.
- **Tier 2 — Ecclesiastical or private membership association.** A form many traditions have used for millennia to protect freedom of assembly and shared practice, carrying certain recognitions without the weight of incorporation.
- **Tier 3 — Fiscal sponsorship.** When you need to receive and steward outside resources but do not yet need your own entity, an aligned organization can hold the financial infrastructure for you.
- **Tier 4 — Legal stewardship entity.** For sustained, complex work — holding land, employing people, operating enterprises. Cooperatives, perpetual purpose trusts, benefit corporations, community land trusts, FairShares Commons, FSx. The key word is *stewardship*: these exist to steward and allocate resources in service of The Goal, never to accumulate power or wealth for its own sake.
- **Tier 5 — Federated stewardship.** Multiple Companies sharing infrastructure, resources, and governance by agreement. This is how the New Civilization scales — not through central control, but through voluntary federation of self-governing bodies (the work of **[[Step 6 — Collective Governance of the Cosmic Superorganism]]**).

The full teaching lives in [[Properly Structure Your Circles]]. The single rule that governs every rung: **does this structure serve our Purpose?** If yes, keep it. If no, change it. If unsure, start simpler.

## The Internal and the External

A Community Company lives in two worlds at once, and clarity here prevents most of the confusion that sinks young organizations.

- **Internally**, it is a sovereign voluntary association — a body of people united by shared Vision, Values, and a Field of Agreements. This is the real Company. It needs no incorporation to be whole.
- **Externally**, when it must interface with the existing legal and financial world, it puts on a legal form the way a person puts on a coat. The entity is the *membrane* between the sovereign body and the Old World — a buffer that lets resources, contracts, and protections flow across the boundary without compromising what the Company *is*.

## How LIØNSBERG Helps 

You do not have to invent your legal structure alone. LIØNSBERG has gone to great lengths to build **shared legal infrastructure** — prototypical, pre-built structures that lower the cost and complexity for everyone. A community can plug into them rather than reinventing the wheel.

Here is the line that must never blur: **this infrastructure is a service offered *to* your sovereign Company, never a parent placed *over* it.** It functions like a Mother Tree in a forest — deep roots that nourish countless independent trees without owning a single one. **Service flows downward; authority flows upward.** 

The simplest path: when you reach the rung where you need the next level of structure, **ask.**  Do not reinvent the wheel.

## What Success Looks Like

You have completed Step 2 when you can point to:

- [ ] A clear, written answer to *"what does our work actually require?"*
- [ ] A **chosen tier** — including, legitimately, "Tier 0 for now."
- [ ] If you formed an entity: the **purpose lock** that keeps it subordinate to The Goal, recorded in your [[Community Company Charter — Template|Charter]] (Section 7).
- [ ] A named understanding of how this Company relates to the wider LIØNSBERG superorganism — *or* a conscious decision that it does not yet.
- [ ] A **review trigger** — the event that will tell you it is time to revisit your form. (a first lease, a first $10,000, a first legal agreement)

If you have these, your Company can act in the world. That is the purpose of this step.
