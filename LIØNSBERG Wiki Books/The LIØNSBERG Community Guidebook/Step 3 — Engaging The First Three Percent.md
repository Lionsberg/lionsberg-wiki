# Step 3 — Engaging The First Three Percent

*You do not need everyone. You need the first few out of every hundred to rise.*

> **The work of this step:** identify and invite the first ~300 people (3% of 10,000) whose wisdom and commitment will ignite the whole community. Not the easiest to reach, and not the most prominent — the **first to rise.**

---

## Why Three Percent

You will never begin by persuading the whole community, and you do not need to. In every population there is a small minority who already sense the urgency — who feel, before you finish your sentence, that something must change and that they are meant to help. They are roughly **three percent**: about **300 people in a community of 10,000.**

This is not a slogan. It is how living systems tip. A committed three percent, organized and connected, forms a decentralized yet coherent network strong enough to carry the whole. The 300 are not the end state — they are the seed crystal. Once they are awake and working, the community follows: the 3% awakens the many, and the many awaken the rest. Your task in this step is only the first link in that chain — to find and gather the 300.

## Find the Already-Ready — Do Not Convince the Reluctant

This is the hinge of the whole step, so hold it clearly:

**You are not looking for people to convince. You are looking for people who are already awake.** They do not need to be persuaded. They need to be *found.*

Spend your early energy on the ready, not the resistant. Arguing a skeptic into the work costs enormous effort and rarely holds. Finding someone who already feels the call costs a single honest conversation — and they bring their own fire. The reluctant are not your enemy and not your failure; their time simply comes later, drawn in by a community already visibly alive (that is **[[Step 5 — Engaging the Community and Passing the Flame]]**). For now, go to the ones who are leaning in.

**Attraction, not promotion.** You spread by being worth joining, not by campaigning. The 300 are reached the way trust always travels — one relationship at a time, Circle to Circle.

## The Four Qualities to Look For

The first to rise tend to carry four marks (from [[The First Three Percent]]):

- **Awakened Awareness** — they already sense the deeper truths behind the crises, without needing to be argued into them.
- **Commitment to Unity** — they reach across difference rather than deepen it; they can hold "us" larger than their faction.
- **Visionary Leadership** — they lead by example and draw others upward, fostering hope and resilience.
- **Service-Oriented Action** — they act from a commitment to the whole, not from fear, ego, or personal gain.

You are not screening for credentials, wealth, or status. You are recognizing a quality of readiness. Some of your most important 300 will be people the Old World overlooked entirely.

## Reach Them in Waves of Resonance

You do not invite 300 people at once. You light a small number of fires that each light their own. The pattern multiplies outward from the Steering Committee:

| Wave | Who | Rough size |
|---|---|---|
| **Seed** | The Steering Committee | 4–7 |
| **First Circle** | The people each founder already trusts | ~30 |
| **Circle of Circles** | The people *they* trust, in turn | ~100 |
| **The First Three Percent** | The wider already-ready, drawn in by a movement visibly alive | ~300 |

Each person you invite is not a recruit to manage but a new center of invitation. The number that matters most is not how many you have gathered — it is **how many of them have begun inviting others.** When people you never met start arriving because someone *they* trust invited them, the fire has caught.

## Reach Across the Divides

A founding body that draws its 300 from only one side of the community will build a Company that serves only one side. From the start, name the lines your community has been divided by — faith, politics, class, generation, neighborhood, race — and consciously seek a trusted bridge across each. That reaching is not a tactic. It is the first concrete act of the Unity the whole guidebook is for.

## The Invitation Itself

An invitation is always a personal conversation, never a broadcast. A good one does three things: it names the urgency honestly, it offers the Way, and it asks for a yes to one small next step — *come and see* — not a vow to serve forever.

> *"Something is starting here that I think you've been waiting for. A few of us are building a way for our community to actually govern and provide for itself. I thought of you immediately. Will you come sit with us?"*

Adapt it to your own voice and to each person. Sincerity travels; scripts do not. Then let your own story — why *you* rose — do the rest. The fire in lived experience persuades where argument cannot.

## What Success Looks Like

You have completed Step 3 when you can point to:

- [ ] A living list of the **already-ready** in your community, growing rather than fixed.
- [ ] A first wave **personally invited** — in conversation, not by announcement.
- [ ] A **bridge named** across each major division in the community.
- [ ] The first signs of **self-spread** — people inviting others without being asked.
- [ ] Enough gathered and willing that you have a body to **train and put to work** (Step 4).

You will know the step is truly working not when your list is long, but when it is **growing without you.** That is the flame catching. Tend it, and move on to teaching your people how to play.
