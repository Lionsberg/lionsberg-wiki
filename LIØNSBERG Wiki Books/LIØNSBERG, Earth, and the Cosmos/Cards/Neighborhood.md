# Neighborhood

_A cluster of Circles, families, and immediately-adjacent sovereigns. Roughly 15 to 40 souls. The natural sub-scale within both [[Village|Villages]] and [[Community|Communities]] — the people whose porches you can see from your own._

---

A **Neighborhood** is the scale just above the [[Circle]] (3 to 13) and just below the [[Village]] (50 to 150). It is the cluster of 2-5 Circles, the extended kin-group, the few-blocks-around-you, the cluster of households who share the same well or the same path through the meadow.

A Neighborhood is **functional within both Villages and Communities**:

- Within a **Village** of 50-150, a Neighborhood is one of the 3-5 natural sub-clusters by which the Village self-organizes
- Within a **Community** of ~10,000, a Neighborhood is the smallest cell of physical proximity — the few households around your own, the immediate block, the shared courtyard

At every settlement form — urban, suburban, rural, intentional, indigenous — the Neighborhood scale appears as the natural unit of immediate physical neighborliness.

## What The Neighborhood Holds

At ~15-40 people, the Neighborhood is the scale at which:

- **Mutual aid is immediate.** When a child needs watching, a meal needs delivering, a fence needs mending — the Neighborhood is who shows up, often without being asked.
- **Direct local stewardship happens.** The shared alley, the corner garden, the small park, the unofficial gathering spot — the Neighborhood holds them.
- **Emergency response is fastest.** In any local trouble, the Neighborhood is the first responders.
- **Children grow up known.** Each Neighborhood child has 15-40 adults who know their name, watch out for them, and contribute to their formation.
- **Sovereign-to-sovereign accountability still works** at human range — close enough to see each other's eyes, far enough that not every interaction must be ceremonial.

## In LIØNSBERG's Scaling

Between Circle and Village, the Neighborhood is a natural cluster but not always a formal federation tier. Within larger Communities (10,000), the Neighborhood becomes more structurally important — the way a Community of 10,000 actually breaks down into 250-500 Neighborhoods of immediate physical proximity, each holding several Circles.

The federation hierarchy with Neighborhood inserted:

> [[Sovereign]] → [[Circle]] (3-13) → **Neighborhood** (15-40) → [[Village]] (50-150) → Microlocality (~1,000) → [[Community]] (~10,000 with [[The 300]]) → Locality (~100,000) → City / Major Population Center (~1,000,000) → [[Ecoregion]] (~10M) → [[Bioregion]] (~50-100M) → [[Georegional]] → Earth as Biome → ...

Whether the Neighborhood becomes a formal Council-bearing tier depends on the local form. In a tight Village of 100, the Village itself may meet directly without distinct Neighborhood structure. In a large City Community, Neighborhood Councils typically emerge naturally.

## Distinguished From

- **[[Circle]]** (3-13) — intentional, gathered for shared purpose; can include sovereigns who don't live nearby. The Neighborhood is geographic; the Circle is purposive.
- **[[Village]]** (50-150) — at full Village scale, direct knowing of everyone still works. The Neighborhood is a sub-unit within the Village.
- **Household** — a single family or shared dwelling, typically smaller. A Neighborhood holds many Households.
