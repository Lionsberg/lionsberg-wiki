# The Warrior's Vow

The covenant beneath every transmission of Power.

---

## The Covenant Beneath The Vow

The Warrior's Vow is not the foundation. **[[The LIØNSBERG Sacred Covenant]]** is.

> _ØNE Spirit._
> _ØNE Body._
> _ØNE Love._
> _All in service of ØNE and All._

Every Vow taken in LIØNSBERG anchors here. The Healers' Vow. The Teachers' Vow. The Builders' Vow. The Stewards' Vow. Every other [[Guild]] Vow. The Warrior's Vow is the Covenant operationalised for the protective, restorative, and (when required) destructive capacity of the Sacred Warrior facet.

---

## The Vow

The Warrior's Vow is **one**, simply spoken — like the Covenant from which it descends.

> _The Power is not mine._
> _It flows through me from the [[ØNE]] in service of [[All]]._
> _I will not turn it against Life, the innocent, the unwilling, or my own Soul._
> _I answer for every act._
> _I release the Power if I cannot hold this Vow._

This is the Vow taken at every belt level of training. The Vow does not change as the Warrior progresses. **The Warrior progresses into the Vow.** The Vow taken at white belt is the same Vow held at black belt — held with more depth, more subtle understanding, more lived capacity to honour what each line actually requires under pressure.

---

## What Each Line Holds

**The Power is not mine.** The capacity cultivated through training is loaned, not owned. The Warrior is a vessel. The Power flows through. The Warrior who begins to claim the Power as their personal property has begun to drift toward the dark initiate.

**It flows through me from the [[ØNE]] in service of [[All]].** The orientation of the Power is fixed at the source. Not the Warrior's interests. Not the Warrior's tribe's interests. Not even the Warrior Guild's interests. The [[ØNE]] and the rising of [[All]] — these alone authorise the Power's flow.

**I will not turn it against Life, the innocent, the unwilling, or my own Soul.** Four protected:
- **Life itself** — the broader living web that the [[ØNE]] sustains
- **The innocent** — those who have not transgressed and are not engaged in transgression
- **The unwilling** — those who have not consented to the encounter
- **My own Soul** — the trained being's deepest core, which can be damaged from within by misuse of the Power

Power directed against any of these is forfeit power. The Vow's violation costs the Warrior the capacity itself.

**I answer for every act.** Sovereign accountability. The trained Warrior does not hide behind chain of command, behind orders, behind ideology, behind the abstraction of institutional duty. The Warrior who strikes is the Warrior who answers — to the [[Circle]], to the Elder, to the [[Wisdom Council]], to the [[ØNE]].

**I release the Power if I cannot hold this Vow.** The terminal clause. The Warrior who recognises that they are drifting — that ego is reasserting, that vengeance is creeping in, that the Vow's grip is weakening — releases the cultivated Power voluntarily, before it can be misused. This is the rarest discipline. It is also the one that proves the Vow was real.

## What The Vow Is For

Energy can be directed to create or to destroy. Information can be patterned in service of life or in service of parasitism. The training that produces a healer who restores life can produce an operator who extracts it. **The training does not select for orientation. The Vow selects for orientation.**

Across history, every disastrous use of cultivated energetic-martial-spiritual capacity traces back to **the dark initiate** — the being who completed the training and betrayed the Vow. The Pattern is operationally protective: lineages that have preserved the genuine Vow have produced healers, protectors, guardians, peacemakers across millennia. Lineages that lost the Vow (or never had it) have produced tyrants, predators, parasitic gurus, weapons-trained operatives serving destruction. The same training, the same techniques, the same capacities — utterly different uses depending on whether the Vow was taken.

The Vow is **the Field of Agreements between the Warrior and the [[ØNE]]**. Without it, cultivation is dangerous. With it, the Pattern protects the being who walks it.

**The Vow is not optional.**

## The Graduated Belt Progression

The Vow is one. The training that the Vow opens access to is graduated by **belt** — white through black, with degrees beyond. Each belt is one season of the Warrior's walk. The same Vow is renewed at each transition, held more deeply at each level.

**Black belt is earned across seven to ten years of focused commitment** — two to three training sessions per week with the [[Circle]] and the Elder, coupled with daily individual practice. The seven-to-ten-year span reflects honest lineage reality. Some Warriors arrive at black belt closer to Year Seven; some closer to Year Ten; rare beings of unusual dedication and prior preparation may arrive earlier. The belt is conferred when the Warrior's standing has matured to hold what black belt requires, not at a calendar deadline.

- **White belt** — Entry. Days One through Ninety. The Vow taken for the first time. Foundational orientation. A trial period; either side may withdraw at the end of ninety days without dishonour.
- **Yellow belt** — Typically the remainder of Year One. The first full year of disciplined practice. The Vow renewed in [[Circle]] with witnesses.
- **Orange belt** — Typically around Year Two. The energetic anatomy opens further; sensing begins to clarify.
- **Green belt** — Typically around Year Three. The first three-year cycle completes. Interface work deepens.
- **Blue belt** — Typically around Year Four to Five. The transition into active field-holding. The Warrior becomes a participant in [[Sacred Guardianship]].
- **Purple belt** — Typically around Year Five to Six. Deeper Interface work clarifies. Transmission begins to flow through the Warrior to others.
- **Brown belt** — Typically around Year Six to Eight. Approaching full operational standing. Most of the cultivation settling into spontaneous capacity.
- **Black belt** — Typically conferred between Year Seven and Year Ten. The threshold of full embodied operation. The deepest transmission the lineage carries is received. Authorisation to begin training others.

Black belt has **degrees** — the lifetime walk beyond first black. First degree at the threshold. Subsequent degrees mark deeper mastery, deeper transmission, deeper service. Conferred by the lineage when the Warrior's standing has matured into the new depth.

## The Deepest Layer

The deepest Black belt commitment includes one further understanding, spoken at the threshold of full transmission: **the willingness to lay down one's own life in defence of the Vow itself**.

The Warrior has answered, in advance, the question of what they will choose if forced to choose between violating the Vow and dying. The answer is recorded. Some Warriors across the ages have been called to honour that recorded answer in actuality. They are remembered as saints, martyrs, paragons. Their names are carried in the lineage. Those who walk after them train against the standard their lives set.

---

## The Public Dimension

The Vow at every belt is **spoken aloud, in the presence of the [[Circle]] and the Elder**, before the Pattern's witnesses.

Private vows are insufficient. The Warrior's accountability to the unseen [[ØNE]] is supplemented by the visible accountability to the gathered Circle. When the Vow is broken, the Circle holds the Warrior accountable. The Warrior does not get to be the sole judge of their own faithfulness.

A being who knows they are being witnessed walks differently than a being who knows they could rationalise the violation alone. Both kinds of accountability are necessary. Either alone is insufficient.
