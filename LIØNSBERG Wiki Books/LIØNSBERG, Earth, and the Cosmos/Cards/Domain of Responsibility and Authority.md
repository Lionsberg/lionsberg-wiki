# Domain of Responsibility and Authority

The irreducible governance unit of fractal civilization.

The New Civilization does not organize around the **person** (the Old World's failure mode) or around the **institution** (the Old World's other failure mode). The New Civilization organizes around the **Domain** — a bounded sphere within which a defined set of sovereign Beings has both responsibility for what happens AND authority to decide what to do. The Domain of Responsibility and Authority — **DRA** — is the atomic cell.

A DRA has four foundational properties:

- **Stakeholders** — the sovereign Beings who care about what happens in this Domain. Their lives are affected. Their gifts are wanted. Their consent is required for the Domain to function.
- **Commons** — the shared resources, capacities, relationships, and territories the Domain holds in trust. Not owned by anyone; stewarded by the DRA as a whole.
- **Field of Agreements** — the explicit covenant governing how the Domain operates. What behavior is in-bounds; what is out-of-bounds; how disputes are resolved; how members enter and exit.
- **Decision rights** — the authority structure within the Domain. Who decides what. Where consent is required. Where any one stakeholder may veto. Where the DRA acts as a body.

Every voluntary association in the New Civilization — Circle, Hub, Guild, Council, bioregional federation, planetary Grid — is structurally a DRA. The same architecture at every scale. **Holofractal**, as the Pattern Language names it.

The DRA is the operational answer to several Old World failures:

- **Anonymous bureaucracy** — the Old World let institutions operate without identifiable responsibility for specific outcomes. The DRA insists on it: every Domain has named stakeholders accountable to each other.
- **Authority without responsibility** — the Old World concentrated authority at levels that bore none of the consequences (the central bank that doesn't suffer inflation; the politician who doesn't fight the war; the executive who doesn't drink the polluted water). The DRA refuses the separation: those who hold authority in a Domain also bear responsibility for what happens in it.
- **Responsibility without authority** — the Old World loaded responsibility on those it deprived of decision-making power (the parent without resources; the citizen without voice; the worker without ownership). The DRA refuses this separation too: those who bear consequences must have voice in decisions.
- **Boundless mandates** — the Old World let institutions claim authority over everything that touched their concern. The DRA is **bounded**: each Domain has a defined edge. Beyond the edge, other DRAs have authority. The boundary is consciously held, not implicitly assumed.

The DRA also has **decision-rights specificity**. Not every decision in a Domain requires the same threshold:

- **Personal decisions** within the Domain that affect only the deciding stakeholder — the stakeholder decides. No DRA process required.
- **Routine decisions** within the Domain affecting the commons — delegated to chosen coordinators, with the DRA retaining the right to call any decision back for review.
- **Boundary-touching decisions** — decisions that affect what enters or exits the Domain — require explicit DRA process. Often consent-thresholds (not consensus, not majority).
- **Foundational decisions** — decisions that would alter the Field of Agreements itself — require unanimous-consent thresholds or near-unanimous, with explicit [[Honoring Dissent|honoring of dissent]] for the few who do not consent.

These thresholds are documented in the DRA's Field of Agreements. The Sovereign entering the Domain knows what is being asked. Surprise governance — the Old World's bait-and-switch — is structurally impossible.

The DRA scales **upward through federation**, not through hierarchy:

- A Circle DRA federates with other Circle DRAs to form a Hub DRA. The Hub DRA does NOT supersede the Circle DRAs; it sits **alongside** them, holding only the responsibilities the Circles cannot hold alone. The Hub is itself a DRA with its own bounded scope.
- Hub DRAs federate to form bioregional DRAs. Same pattern. The bioregional DRA holds what the Hubs cannot hold alone. It does not absorb the Hubs.
- Bioregional DRAs federate to form the planetary Grid DRA. Same pattern.

At every scale, the DRA's mandate is bounded by [[Subsidiarity and the Swiss Pattern|subsidiarity]]: it only holds what the level below cannot hold. Drift upward — the higher DRA absorbing competences the lower DRA can handle — is the diagnostic sign of imperial corruption and is structurally resisted.

The DRA also has **exit protocols**. A stakeholder who no longer consents to remain within a Domain has the right to withdraw — through [[Withdrawal of Consent and Exodus Protocols]]. The Domain does not collapse; the stakeholder is released; other stakeholders may step into the vacated role. The Domain has procedures for both gentle and adversarial exits.

This is the **architecture** Citizen Led Governance contributes to the Codex. The DRA is the structural form within which the [[The Terms of the New Covenant]] are lived, within which [[The Network of Commitments]] tracks promises, within which [[Throughput of The Goal]] is measured, within which the [[The Quest]] is undertaken. Without the DRA architecture, the Pattern has nowhere to live operationally. With it, the Pattern has its **organ**.

Lives canonically in [[The LIØNSBERG Pattern Language]] as a foundational Pattern within Element 4 (Governance and Wise Eldership) and Element 2 (Holofractal Omnifederation).
