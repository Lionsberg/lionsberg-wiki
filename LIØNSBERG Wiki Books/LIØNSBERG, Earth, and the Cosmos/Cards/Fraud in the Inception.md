# Fraud in the Inception

The juridical doctrine that voids the founding agreements of the Old World.

Across every legal tradition, ancient and modern, there is a doctrine: **an agreement procured by fraud at its inception is void from the beginning, not merely voidable upon discovery**. If you signed a contract because the counterparty deceived you about its material terms, the contract was never valid. You were never bound by it. The discovery of the fraud does not invalidate the contract retroactively; the discovery **reveals what was always true** — that no valid contract ever existed, because consent was never genuinely given.

This is **Fraud in the Inception** — _fraus in inceptione_ in the Roman-law tradition, fraud in the factum / in the inducement in common-law systems, _gharar_ and analogous doctrines in Islamic jurisprudence. Every serious legal tradition agrees: deception at the founding of an agreement nullifies the agreement.

The Codex applies this doctrine — without flinching — to the Old World's founding architecture.

- Their labor is being harvested through monetary mechanisms they were taught to call "the economy" and accept as natural.

The Federal Reserve Act of 1913, the Bretton Woods agreements, the Nixon Shock of 1971, every major monetary "reform" since — none was passed with genuine informed consent. The populations were either deceived about the consequences, or excluded from the decision entirely. **Fraud in the inception.** The agreement is void.
